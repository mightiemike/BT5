### Title
Bad Debt Spot Borrow Positions Continue to Accrue Interest, Crediting Unbacked Protocol Fees — (File: `core/contracts/SpotEngineState.sol`)

---

### Summary

When a subaccount's spot borrow becomes bad debt — maintenance health negative, insurance exhausted, and normal liquidation blocked — the position remains in `totalBorrowsNormalized` and continues to accrue interest through every `_updateState` call. This inflates `utilizationRatioX18`, drives up borrow rates for all borrowers, and credits phantom protocol fees to `FEES_ACCOUNT`. Because there is no liquidator incentive to finalize a pure-liability subaccount, bad debt can persist indefinitely, continuously generating unbacked fee credits that corrupt the SpotEngine's solvency accounting.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause — `_updateState` does not exclude bad debt from interest accrual**

`SpotEngineState._updateState` computes interest for every product uniformly:

```solidity
int128 totalBorrows = state.totalBorrowsNormalized.mul(
    state.cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18
);
int128 utilizationRatioX18 = totalBorrows.div(totalDeposits);
```

`totalBorrowsNormalized` includes every borrower — healthy or insolvent. There is no mechanism to exclude bad debt positions. [1](#0-0) 

The borrow rate multiplier and the protocol fee amount are both derived from this inflated utilization:

```solidity
int128 totalDepositRateX18 = utilizationRatioX18.mul(borrowRateMultiplierX18 - ONE);
int128 realizedDepositRateX18 = totalDepositRateX18.mul(ONE - INTEREST_FEE_FRACTION);
int128 feesAmt = totalDeposits.mul(totalDepositRateX18 - realizedDepositRateX18);
``` [2](#0-1) 

`feesAmt` is then credited to `FEES_ACCOUNT` as a deposit balance: [3](#0-2) 

**How bad debt arises and becomes stuck**

In `ClearinghouseLiq._assertLiquidationAmount`, when a subaccount has a negative spot balance (liability), the maximum liquidatable amount is bounded by the subaccount's quote balance plus the insurance fund:

```solidity
int128 maxBuy = (quoteBalance.amount + insurance).div(liquidationPrice);
maxBuy = MathHelper.max(maxBuy + 1, 0);
maxLiquidatable = MathHelper.max(spotAmount, -maxBuy);
``` [4](#0-3) 

When `quoteBalance.amount + insurance` is deeply negative (insurance exhausted, quote deeply negative), `maxBuy` evaluates to `0`, making `maxLiquidatable = 0`. Any non-zero liquidation amount then fails the assertion, completely blocking normal liquidation. [5](#0-4) 

The alternative path — `_finalizeSubaccount` with `txn.productId == type(uint32).max` — can socialize the loss, but it requires a liquidator to submit the transaction. For a pure-liability subaccount (no assets, only negative balances), the liquidator receives nothing: [6](#0-5) 

There is no on-chain incentive (no reward, no seized asset) for a liquidator to pay gas to finalize a zero-asset subaccount. The bad debt position therefore persists indefinitely.

**The accounting corruption**

With the bad debt position stuck, every sequencer-triggered `updateStates` call continues to:

1. Include the bad debt borrow in `totalBorrows`, inflating `utilizationRatioX18`
2. Grow `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` — the bad debt borrower's effective liability increases, but they will never repay
3. Grow `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` — depositors receive phantom yield funded by interest that will never be paid
4. Credit `feesAmt` to `FEES_ACCOUNT` — protocol fees partially backed by phantom interest [7](#0-6) 

When socialization eventually occurs (if ever triggered), `SpotEngine.socializeSubaccount` reduces `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`:

```solidity
state.cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18 = (totalDeposited + balance.amount)
    .div(state.totalDepositsNormalized);
``` [8](#0-7) 

This haircut is **larger than it would have been** had bad debt interest not accrued, because the multiplier was artificially inflated during the bad debt period. If protocol fees were withdrawn from `FEES_ACCOUNT` during that period, those withdrawals are unbacked — the socialization haircut falls entirely on remaining depositors.

---

### Impact Explanation

- **Depositors** receive phantom yield (inflated `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`) that is later clawed back via a larger-than-expected socialization haircut.
- **Protocol fees** (`FEES_ACCOUNT`) are credited with phantom interest from bad debt borrowers. If withdrawn before socialization, these fees are unbacked — the loss is borne entirely by depositors, not the protocol.
- **All healthy borrowers** pay higher interest rates than warranted because bad debt inflates `utilizationRatioX18`, increasing `borrowRateMultiplierX18` for everyone.
- The longer bad debt persists without finalization, the larger the compounding distortion to the SpotEngine's accounting invariants.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Bad debt arises naturally during rapid asset price crashes when the insurance fund is insufficient. The absence of any liquidator incentive to finalize pure-liability subaccounts (no seized assets, only gas cost) means bad debt positions are likely to persist for extended periods. The sequencer calls `updateStates` continuously, so interest accrual on bad debt is automatic and ongoing — no attacker action is required.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Track bad debt separately**: Add a `totalBadDebtNormalized` variable per product. When a position is identified as unliquidatable (normal liquidation blocked), move its normalized borrow amount out of `totalBorrowsNormalized` and into `totalBadDebtNormalized`.

2. **Exclude bad debt from utilization**: In `_updateState`, compute `utilizationRatioX18` using only `totalBorrowsNormalized - totalBadDebtNormalized`, so healthy borrowers are not penalized and `feesAmt` is not inflated by phantom interest.

3. **Add finalization incentive**: Provide a small reward (from the insurance fund or a protocol reserve) to liquidators who call `_finalizeSubaccount` on zero-asset bad debt subaccounts, ensuring timely socialization.

4. **Auto-finalize**: Consider allowing the sequencer to automatically trigger `_finalizeSubaccount` for subaccounts where `maxLiquidatable == 0` and maintenance health is negative, removing the dependency on external liquidator action.

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:**
- Product: ETH spot (productId = 2), USDC quote (productId = 0)
- Alice borrows 1,000 ETH worth of USDC (negative spot balance)
- Insurance fund: 0 (exhausted by prior events)

**Step 1 — Bad debt arises:**
ETH price crashes 90%. Alice's maintenance health goes negative. Her quote balance is −500 USDC, insurance = 0.

**Step 2 — Normal liquidation is blocked:**
`_assertLiquidationAmount` computes:
```
maxBuy = max((-500 + 0) / liquidationPrice + 1, 0) = max(negative + 1, 0) = 0
maxLiquidatable = max(spotAmount, -0) = max(-1000, 0) = 0
```
Any `txn.amount != 0` fails `require(maxLiquidatable <= txn.amount)`. [9](#0-8) 

**Step 3 — No finalization incentive:**
Alice has no assets. A liquidator calling `_finalizeSubaccount` receives nothing and pays gas. No liquidator acts.

**Step 4 — Interest accrues indefinitely:**
The sequencer calls `updateStates` every epoch. `_updateState` includes Alice's borrow in `totalBorrows`, inflating `utilizationRatioX18`. `feesAmt` is credited to `FEES_ACCOUNT` each epoch. [10](#0-9) 

**Step 5 — Fees are withdrawn:**
The protocol withdraws accumulated `FEES_ACCOUNT` balance. These fees are partially backed by Alice's phantom interest.

**Step 6 — Socialization (eventually):**
A liquidator finally calls `_finalizeSubaccount`. `SpotEngine.socializeSubaccount` reduces `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` to cover Alice's liability. The haircut is larger than it would have been without the phantom interest accrual. The withdrawn fees are not clawed back. All remaining depositors bear the full loss. [11](#0-10)
