### Title
Zero-Weight Spot Asset Bypass in `_assertCanLiquidateLiability` Allows Insurance Fund Drain While Liquidatee Retains Real-Value Assets — (`core/contracts/ClearinghouseLiq.sol`)

---

### Summary

`_assertCanLiquidateLiability` explicitly skips spot products whose `longWeightInitialX18 == 0` when checking that all spot assets are exhausted before liability liquidation. Because those same products are also blocked from being liquidated by a symmetric guard in `_assertLiquidationAmount`, the protocol creates an irresolvable situation: the liquidatee can hold a positive balance in a zero-weight spot product indefinitely, yet the liability-liquidation gate passes, allowing insurance funds to cover the USDC deficit while the liquidatee retains the unweighted asset.

These two guards are mutually reinforcing: the asset cannot be liquidated, and the liability gate ignores it. The result is that a liquidatee holding a positive balance in a zero-weight spot product (e.g., a token whose `longWeightInitial` was set to `0` via `addOrUpdateProduct` after the user deposited) can never be forced to sell it, yet the liability-liquidation path proceeds as if all spot assets have been exhausted.

`longWeightInitialX18` and `longWeightMaintenanceX18` are independent storage fields (`RiskStore.longWeightInitial` / `longWeightMaintenance`). A product can have `longWeightInitial == 0` while `longWeightMaintenance == 0` as well, meaning the asset contributes **zero** to both initial and maintenance health. The account can therefore be under maintenance health (`isUnderMaintenance` returns true) despite holding a positive balance in this product, satisfying the entry guard at line 603. [6](#0-5) 

The same skip pattern appears identically in `_finalizeSubaccount` (lines 303–305 and 379–381), confirming it is a deliberate design choice — but one that creates the value-transfer path. [7](#0-6)

### Impact Explanation

**Exact asset delta:**

| Party | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Liquidatee | +A zero-weight token, −L spot liability, −Q USDC | +A zero-weight token, 0 liability, 0 USDC |
| Insurance pool | `insurance` | `insurance − Q` |

The liquidatee retains the zero-weight asset (which has a non-zero `priceX18` and is withdrawable via `withdrawCollateral` once initial health ≥ 0 after liabilities are cleared) while the insurance pool absorbs the USDC shortfall. This is a direct, incorrect transfer of value from the insurance pool to the liquidatee, matching the Critical scope: *"transaction manipulation that changes liquidation terms or settlement outcomes in a way that transfers value incorrectly."*

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The precondition — a spot product with `longWeightInitial == 0` and a non-zero `priceX18` — is reachable through the normal governance path: `addOrUpdateProduct` can set `longWeightInitial` to `0` on an existing product (e.g., during a risk-parameter update or deprecation), while users who deposited before the change retain positive balances. No privileged attacker role is required; any liquidator can trigger the path once the liquidatee is under maintenance health.

---

### Recommendation

Two complementary fixes:

1. **Remove the skip in `_assertCanLiquidateLiability`**: Check all non-quote spot balances regardless of weight. If a zero-weight product has a positive balance, revert with `ERR_NOT_LIQUIDATABLE_LIABILITIES`. This enforces the invariant that all spot assets must be exhausted before insurance is used.

2. **Allow liquidation of zero-weight spot assets**: Remove or relax the `longWeightInitialX18 != 0` guard in `_assertLiquidationAmount` for the liability-liquidation path, so liquidators can clear zero-weight assets before proceeding to liability buyback.

Either fix alone closes the gap; both together are the most robust solution.
