### Title
Whale NLP Holder Can Manipulate Quote Utilization to Extract Excess Interest — (`core/contracts/SpotEngineState.sol`)

---

### Summary

A whale who holds a majority of NLP tokens can open offsetting deposit/borrow positions in the quote asset across two subaccounts to artificially inflate the quote utilization ratio. Because the `SpotEngine` interest rate is a direct function of utilization, this forces all other quote borrowers to pay higher interest. Since the whale's NLP represents a majority claim on the NLP pool subaccounts — which are the primary quote depositors — most of the inflated interest flows back to the whale, making the attack net-profitable and largely risk-free.

---

### Finding Description

The `SpotEngine` uses a kinked utilization-based interest rate model. The borrow rate is computed in `_updateState` as:

```solidity
int128 utilizationRatioX18 = totalBorrows.div(totalDeposits);
``` [1](#0-0) 

The resulting borrow rate multiplier is applied to `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18`, and the deposit rate (minus the 20% `INTEREST_FEE_FRACTION` protocol cut) is applied to `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`: [2](#0-1) 

NLP pool subaccounts hold `QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID` balances, credited via `_applyNlpRebalance` during every `mintNlp` call:

```solidity
spotEngine.updateBalance(QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID, nlpPools[i].subaccount, nlpPoolRebalanceX18[i]);
``` [3](#0-2) 

Because NLP pool subaccounts are quote depositors, their balances grow with `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`. The NLP oracle price (set by the sequencer) is expected to reflect this growing pool value, so NLP holders benefit directly from higher deposit interest.

**Attack path:**

1. Whale holds majority NLP (e.g., 60% of total NLP supply), meaning NLP pool subaccounts they are entitled to hold ~60% of all quote deposits.
2. Whale opens **subaccount A** and deposits a large amount of quote (`+X`), increasing `totalDepositsNormalized`.
3. Whale opens **subaccount B** with sufficient collateral (e.g., NLP itself, if it carries positive risk weights) and borrows the same amount of quote (`−X`), increasing `totalBorrowsNormalized`.
4. Because the pre-attack utilization `B/D < 1`, adding equal `X` to both numerator and denominator raises utilization: `(B+X)/(D+X) > B/D`.
5. The next `updateStates` call computes a higher `borrowerRateX18`, increasing interest paid by **all** quote borrowers.
6. 80% of that interest accrues to quote depositors (NLP pool subaccounts). The whale's ~60% NLP share captures ~60% of all new interest.
7. The whale's own borrow cost (on `X`) is offset by the interest they receive on `X` deposited in subaccount A, plus the disproportionate share of interest extracted from other borrowers.
8. When the attack is no longer profitable (e.g., other LPs enter), the whale closes both positions at no directional loss.

The `QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID` interest rate configuration confirms the quote asset is subject to this kinked model: [4](#0-3) 

The `INTEREST_FEE_FRACTION` constant (20%) confirms only 80% of interest flows to depositors, but this does not prevent the attack: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

All quote borrowers (traders with leveraged spot positions or negative quote balances) pay artificially elevated interest rates for the duration of the attack. This is a direct, quantifiable loss of funds for honest protocol users. The whale extracts the excess interest through their NLP holdings, representing a wealth transfer from borrowers to the attacker.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires a majority NLP stake. In early-stage or low-liquidity deployments this is realistic — a single large depositor can easily dominate the NLP pool. The 4-day NLP lock period (`NLP_LOCK_PERIOD`) does not prevent the attack; it only delays exit. The offsetting deposit/borrow positions carry no directional market risk, making the attack sustainable indefinitely as long as the whale maintains majority NLP. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Recommendation

1. **Decouple NLP holder identity from borrower identity**: Prevent a subaccount (or address) that holds NLP above a threshold from also holding large borrow positions in the quote asset.
2. **Time-weighted or smoothed utilization**: Compute the interest rate from a time-weighted average utilization rather than the instantaneous snapshot, making single-block manipulation unprofitable.
3. **Utilization caps per subaccount**: Limit the fraction of `totalBorrows` that any single address (across all its subaccounts) can contribute, preventing artificial inflation.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
Initial state:
  totalDeposits (quote) = 10,000,000 USDC  (NLP pool subaccounts hold ~6,000,000)
  totalBorrows  (quote) =  2,000,000 USDC
  utilizationRatio      = 20%
  borrowerRate          ≈ floor + small_cap * (0.20 / 0.80) = low rate

Whale attack (X = 5,000,000 USDC):
  subaccount A deposits  +5,000,000  → totalDeposits = 15,000,000
  subaccount B borrows   -5,000,000  → totalBorrows  =  7,000,000
  new utilizationRatio   = 7,000,000 / 15,000,000 ≈ 46.7%  (was 20%)
  new borrowerRate       >> old rate (past inflection point at 80%)

Interest extracted per epoch:
  All 7,000,000 USDC of borrows pay the elevated rate.
  Whale pays rate on their own 5,000,000 borrow.
  Whale receives ~60% of deposit interest on 15,000,000 deposits via NLP.
  Net: whale profits from the 2,000,000 USDC of pre-existing borrows
       paying the elevated rate, with ~60% of that interest flowing to them.
```

The root cause is `_updateState` in `SpotEngineState.sol` computing utilization from instantaneous totals with no manipulation guard: [7](#0-6)
