### Title
Wrong Price Formula in Spread Health Adjustment Uses `spotPrice + perpPrice` Instead of `spotPrice - perpPrice` — (File: `core/contracts/Clearinghouse.sol`)

---

### Summary

In `Clearinghouse.getHealth`, the spread health adjustment multiplies `basisAmount` by `spotCoreRisk.price + perpCoreRisk.price`. The correct formula for a hedged spread position's net price exposure is `spotPrice - perpPrice` (the basis). Using the sum instead of the difference inflates the health bonus by approximately `2 × spotPrice / basis`, allowing long-spread holders to appear far healthier than they are and borrow excess collateral or evade liquidation.

---

### Finding Description

`Clearinghouse.getHealth` computes a spread health bonus to reward hedged positions (long spot + short perp, or vice versa). The adjustment is:

```solidity
health += basisAmount
    .mul(spotCoreRisk.price + perpCoreRisk.price)
    .mul(spreadWeight - existingWeight);
``` [1](#0-0) 

The net price exposure of a spread position is `spotPrice − perpPrice` (the basis), because the long leg gains when spot rises and the short leg loses when perp rises. The correct delta between spread-adjusted health and the already-counted individual health is:

```
basisAmount × (spotPrice − perpPrice) × (spreadWeight − existingWeight)
```

The code uses `spotPrice + perpPrice` instead of `spotPrice − perpPrice`. Since spot and perp prices track the same underlying asset, `spotPrice ≈ perpPrice`, so:

- `spotPrice − perpPrice ≈ 0` (small basis, correct)
- `spotPrice + perpPrice ≈ 2 × spotPrice` (large, wrong)

The health bonus is therefore inflated by a factor of roughly `2 × spotPrice / basis`.

`spreadWeight` is always `≥ existingWeight` for a long spread (both are ≤ 1, but `spreadWeight` is closer to 1), so `spreadWeight − existingWeight ≥ 0`. [2](#0-1) 

`existingWeight` is computed using `longWeight` for both legs, even though the perp leg in a long spread is short (and thus uses `shortPerpWeight ≥ 1` in the actual health calculation). This further underestimates `existingWeight`, compounding the overstatement of `spreadWeight − existingWeight`. [3](#0-2) 

The `getCoreRisk` function always passes `amount = 1` to `_getWeightX18`, so `longWeight` is returned regardless of the actual position direction. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

`getHealth` is called in every safety-critical path:

- `withdrawCollateral` — checks `getHealth(sender, INITIAL) >= 0` after reducing balance. [5](#0-4) 
- `transferQuote` — calls `_isAboveInitial`. [6](#0-5) 
- `mintNlp` / `burnNlp` — both call `getHealth`. [7](#0-6) 
- `liquidateSubaccount` → `isUnderMaintenance` → `getHealth`. [8](#0-7) 

An attacker holding a long spread position receives a health bonus proportional to `2 × spotPrice` instead of the near-zero basis. This allows:

1. **Over-borrowing**: Withdraw collateral far beyond what the position actually supports.
2. **Liquidation evasion**: Appear healthy when the position is genuinely under-collateralized.
3. **Short spread victims**: Users with short spread positions receive a large *negative* adjustment (since `basisAmount < 0`), causing premature liquidation of legitimately healthy positions.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Spread positions (long spot + short perp) are a standard, incentivized strategy in perpetual DEXes (basis trading, funding-rate arbitrage). Any user who opens such a position is immediately affected. No special privilege or admin access is required — the attacker only needs to submit a `matchOrders` transaction through the `Endpoint` to establish the spread, then call `withdrawCollateral` or `transferQuote` to extract excess value.

---

### Recommendation

Change line 134 in `Clearinghouse.sol` from:

```solidity
health += basisAmount
    .mul(spotCoreRisk.price + perpCoreRisk.price)
    .mul(spreadWeight - existingWeight);
```

to:

```solidity
health += basisAmount
    .mul(spotCoreRisk.price - perpCoreRisk.price)
    .mul(spreadWeight - existingWeight);
```

Additionally, `existingWeight` should account for the actual direction of each leg (using `shortPerpWeight` for the short perp leg in a long spread) to avoid a secondary overstatement.

---

### Proof of Concept

Assume BTC spot and perp both priced at `$50,000`, maintenance long weight `0.9`, maintenance short weight `1.1`.

**User opens long spread**: long 1 BTC spot, short 1 BTC perp.

- `basisAmount = 1e18`
- `spotPrice = perpPrice = 50_000e18`
- `existingWeight = (0.9e18 + 0.9e18) / 2 = 0.9e18`
- `spreadWeight = ONE − (ONE − 0.9e18) / 5 = 0.98e18`
- `spreadWeight − existingWeight = 0.08e18`

**Correct health bonus** (using `spotPrice − perpPrice`):
```
1e18 × (50_000e18 − 50_000e18) × 0.08e18 = 0
```

**Actual health bonus** (using `spotPrice + perpPrice`):
```
1e18 × (50_000e18 + 50_000e18) × 0.08e18
= 1e18 × 100_000e18 × 0.08e18
= 8_000e36  →  $8,000 of phantom health
```

The attacker receives `$8,000` of phantom health per BTC of spread notional, allowing them to withdraw `$8,000` more collateral than the position actually supports. Scaling to 10 BTC gives `$80,000` of phantom health — a direct, unprivileged drain of protocol collateral.
