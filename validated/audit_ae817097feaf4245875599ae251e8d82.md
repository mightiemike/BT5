### Title
`submit_blocks` Is Permanently Blocked for `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` Holders When Paused Due to Mismatched Pause Guard on Internal `run_mainchain_gc` Call — (`contract/src/lib.rs`)

---

### Summary

`submit_blocks` is designed to remain callable by accounts holding `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` even when the contract is paused. However, it unconditionally calls `self.run_mainchain_gc(...)` internally, and `run_mainchain_gc` carries a separate, incompatible pause guard — `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]` — that only exempts `Role::UnrestrictedRunGC` holders. Because these are distinct roles, any trusted relayer or account granted only `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` will have every `submit_blocks` call revert at the internal GC step when the contract is paused. The light client cannot be kept synchronized by authorized relayers during a pause.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause — mismatched pause-bypass roles across caller and callee:**

`submit_blocks` is decorated with `#[pause]` and `#[trusted_relayer]`: [1](#0-0) 

The `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role is explicitly documented as the bypass that lets callers use the contract API while paused: [2](#0-1) 

Inside `submit_blocks`, after processing all headers, the function unconditionally calls: [3](#0-2) 

`run_mainchain_gc` carries its own, independent pause guard: [4](#0-3) 

The bypass role for `run_mainchain_gc` is `Role::UnrestrictedRunGC`: [5](#0-4) 

These are two separate enum variants with no overlap: [6](#0-5) 

**Why the internal call triggers the guard:**

In NEAR, `self.run_mainchain_gc(...)` is a direct Rust method call, not a cross-contract call. The `#[pause(except(roles(...)))]` macro inserts a guard at the top of `run_mainchain_gc` that reads `env::predecessor_account_id()` — which remains the original external caller throughout the entire execution frame. When the contract is paused and the caller holds only `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks`, the guard in `run_mainchain_gc` checks for `UnrestrictedRunGC`, finds it absent, and panics. The entire `submit_blocks` transaction reverts.

**The desynchronization:**

| State | `submit_blocks` outer guard | `run_mainchain_gc` inner guard | Net result for `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` holder |
|---|---|---|---|
| Not paused | passes | passes | works |
| Paused | passes (bypass role) | **fails** (wrong bypass role) | **reverts** |

---

### Impact Explanation

When the contract is paused, trusted relayers — the primary production actors responsible for keeping the on-chain Bitcoin header chain synchronized — cannot submit any blocks. Every call to `submit_blocks` reverts at the `run_mainchain_gc` step regardless of how many headers are in the batch. The light client tip freezes at the height it was at when the pause began. Downstream NEAR contracts calling `verify_transaction_inclusion` or `verify_transaction_inclusion_v2` will either fail (block not in mainchain) or return results against a stale chain tip, undermining the SPV security guarantee. The longer the pause, the larger the synchronization gap and the greater the downstream impact.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The trigger requires only two ordinary operational conditions that co-occur routinely:

1. A `PauseManager` pauses the contract (maintenance, incident response, upgrade staging).
2. A trusted relayer with `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` attempts to submit headers — the exact scenario the role was designed for.

No adversarial input is needed. Any trusted relayer calling `submit_blocks` during a pause will hit this unconditionally. The `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role exists precisely to keep the light client live during pauses, so this scenario is an expected operational path, not an edge case.

---

### Recommendation

Remove the `#[pause(...)]` guard from `run_mainchain_gc` and replace it with an internal-only visibility pattern, or add `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` to the except clause of `run_mainchain_gc`'s pause guard so that callers authorized to submit blocks are also authorized to trigger GC:

```rust
// Option A: extend the except clause
#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC, Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks)))]
pub fn run_mainchain_gc(&mut self, batch_size: u64) { ... }
```

Alternatively, split `run_mainchain_gc` into a public entry point (with its own pause guard) and a private `run_mainchain_gc_inner` that carries no guard, and have `submit_blocks` call the private variant directly. This eliminates the double-guard problem entirely and makes the access model explicit.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Preconditions:**
- Contract is deployed and initialized with a valid Bitcoin genesis block.
- Account `relayer.near` is registered as a trusted relayer and granted `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` but **not** `Role::UnrestrictedRunGC`.
- `PauseManager` pauses the contract.

**Steps:**
1. `relayer.near` calls `submit_blocks` with a valid batch of Bitcoin headers and sufficient attached deposit.
2. The `#[pause]` guard on `submit_blocks` passes because `relayer.near` holds `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks`.
3. The `#[trusted_relayer]` check passes because `relayer.near` is a registered relayer.
4. All headers are processed successfully via `submit_block_header`.
5. Execution reaches line 181: `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)`.
6. The `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]` guard fires. `env::predecessor_account_id()` is `relayer.near`. `relayer.near` does not hold `UnrestrictedRunGC`. The guard panics.
7. The entire transaction reverts. No headers are committed. The light client tip is unchanged.

**Expected (correct) behavior:** Headers are committed and GC runs, keeping the light client synchronized during the pause.

**Actual behavior:** Every `submit_blocks` call reverts. The light client cannot advance while paused, even for authorized relayers.
