### Title
Division by Zero in `socializeSubaccount` When `openInterest` Is Zero — (`File: core/contracts/PerpEngine.sol`)

---

### Summary

`PerpEngine.socializeSubaccount` unconditionally divides by `state.openInterest` when distributing a liquidatee's residual negative `vQuoteBalance` across all open-interest holders. If `state.openInterest` is zero for a given product at the time of socialization — a reachable state when the liquidatee is the sole remaining participant with a non-zero position history — the call reverts with `"DBZ"`, permanently blocking finalization of that subaccount and freezing the bad-debt recovery path.

---

### Finding Description

In `PerpEngine.socializeSubaccount`, after insurance is applied, any remaining negative `vQuoteBalance` is spread across all open-interest holders via:

```solidity
int128 fundingPerShare = -balance.vQuoteBalance.div(
    state.openInterest   // ← can be zero
);
``` [1](#0-0) 

`state.openInterest` is the protocol-wide sum of absolute position sizes for that product, maintained in `_updateBalance`:

```solidity
state.openInterest -= balance.amount.abs();   // pre-update
...
state.openInterest += balance.amount;          // post-update (long)
state.openInterest -= balance.amount;          // post-update (short)
``` [2](#0-1) 

`socializeSubaccount` is called exclusively from `_finalizeSubaccount` in `ClearinghouseLiq`, which first enforces that **all** of the liquidatee's perp `amount` fields are zero before proceeding:

```solidity
require(balance.amount == 0, ERR_NOT_FINALIZABLE_SUBACCOUNT);
``` [3](#0-2) 

Because the liquidatee's position has already been fully closed (amount = 0), their contribution to `openInterest` is zero. If no other participant holds an open position in that product, `state.openInterest == 0` globally. The liquidatee can still carry a negative `vQuoteBalance` (realized loss from the closed position) that was not fully covered by their quote balance or the insurance fund. When `socializeSubaccount` reaches that product, the division at line 166 reverts. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

The revert inside `socializeSubaccount` propagates up through `_finalizeSubaccount` and through the `delegatecall` in `Clearinghouse.liquidateSubaccount`, causing the entire liquidation transaction to revert. [5](#0-4) 

Concrete corrupted state:
- The liquidatee's subaccount cannot be finalized. Its negative `vQuoteBalance` is never zeroed out and never socialized.
- The insurance fund cannot be applied to cover the shortfall (the call reverts before `insurance` is updated).
- The bad debt is permanently stuck: no liquidator can complete the finalization, and the protocol's socialization mechanism is broken for that product until other participants re-open positions.

This is not a gas-only or theoretical issue: it blocks a critical protocol invariant (insolvent accounts must be resolvable) and leaves unrecoverable bad debt in the system.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The condition requires:
1. A perp product where all participants (other than the liquidatee) have closed their positions — `state.openInterest == 0`.
2. The liquidatee has `balance.amount == 0` (enforced by `_finalizeSubaccount`) but `balance.vQuoteBalance < 0` (residual realized loss).
3. Insurance is insufficient to fully cover the negative `vQuoteBalance`.

This is realistic in:
- Newly listed perp products with low participation.
- Products where all other traders have exited before the liquidatee is finalized.
- Isolated subaccounts in niche markets.

No privileged access is required. Any liquidator submitting a finalization transaction (`productId == type(uint32).max`) against a qualifying subaccount triggers the path. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Recommendation

Guard the socialization division with a zero-check on `state.openInterest`, mirroring the pattern already used in `PerpEngineState.updateStates`:

```solidity
if (state.openInterest == 0) {
    continue;
}
``` [7](#0-6) 

If `openInterest` is zero, there are no open-interest holders to socialize against. The residual loss should either be absorbed by the insurance fund entirely, or the negative `vQuoteBalance` should be zeroed and the loss recorded separately. At minimum, the division must be skipped to prevent a permanent revert.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup**: One perp product (productId = 2). Insurance fund = 0.

1. **Alice** opens a long position of 10 units. `state.openInterest = 10`.
2. Price drops. Alice's position PnL is −100 USDC.
3. **Alice** closes her position via normal trading. `balance.amount = 0`, `balance.vQuoteBalance = −100`, `state.openInterest = 0`.
4. Alice's quote balance is 0 (she had no collateral beyond the position).
5. Alice's maintenance health < 0 → she is liquidatable.
6. A liquidator calls `liquidateSubaccount` with `productId = type(uint32).max` (finalization).
7. `_finalizeSubaccount` confirms `balance.amount == 0` ✓, settles positive PnL (none), settles negative PnL against quote (quote = 0, no settlement), then calls `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(alice, 0)`.
8. Inside `socializeSubaccount`: `balance.vQuoteBalance = −100 < 0`, insurance = 0, so `balance.vQuoteBalance` remains −100 after insurance step.
9. `state.openInterest = 0` → `(-(-100)).div(0)` → **revert "DBZ"**.
10. The entire liquidation transaction reverts. Alice's account can never be finalized. [8](#0-7) [9](#0-8)
