### Title
Negative `v.insurance` in `_finalizeSubaccount` Corrupts `availableSettle` and Amplifies Socialized Losses — (`core/contracts/ClearinghouseLiq.sol` / `core/contracts/PerpEngine.sol`)

---

### Summary

`_finalizeSubaccount` computes `v.insurance = insurance - lastLiquidationFees` without a non-negativity guard. If `insurance` has been drained below `lastLiquidationFees` between the last liquidation step and the finalization call, `v.insurance` is negative. This negative value is passed directly to `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount`, where `MathHelper.min(negative_insurance, positive_value)` returns the negative insurance as `insuranceCover`, causing `state.availableSettle` to **decrease** and `balance.vQuoteBalance` to become **more negative**, amplifying the amount subsequently socialized across all other participants.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — `lastLiquidationFees` is a global, not per-subaccount, storage variable.** [1](#0-0) 

Both `insurance` and `lastLiquidationFees` are single global `int128` slots shared across all liquidations.

**Step 2 — `_handleLiquidationPayment` sets the global `lastLiquidationFees` and adds fees to `insurance`.** [2](#0-1) 

After this call: `insurance = I_prev + F` and `lastLiquidationFees = F`, so `insurance >= lastLiquidationFees` holds immediately after.

**Step 3 — Between transactions, `updateQuoteFromInsurance` can drain `insurance` toward zero.** [3](#0-2) 

`updateQuoteFromInsurance` clamps the drain to `max(..., 0)`, so `insurance` can reach 0 but not go negative. If `I_prev` was small (e.g., 0), then after any subsequent liquidation drains even 1 unit, `insurance < F = lastLiquidationFees`.

**Step 4 — `_finalizeSubaccount` subtracts `lastLiquidationFees` with no guard.** [4](#0-3) 

`v.insurance` is now negative. It is passed directly to `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount`: [5](#0-4) 

**Step 5 — `socializeSubaccount` applies the negative insurance, corrupting `availableSettle`.** [6](#0-5) 

When `insurance < 0` and `balance.vQuoteBalance < 0`:
- `-balance.vQuoteBalance > 0`
- `MathHelper.min(negative, positive)` returns the **negative** value as `insuranceCover`
- `state.availableSettle += insuranceCover` → **decreases** `availableSettle`
- `balance.vQuoteBalance += insuranceCover` → becomes **more negative** than before socialization

The more-negative `vQuoteBalance` then enters the socialization block at lines 164–171, spreading a **larger** loss across all open-interest holders than actually existed. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Two simultaneous Critical impacts:

1. **Permanent lock of PnL** — `state.availableSettle` is permanently reduced. `getSettlementState` caps settleable PnL at `min(positionPnl, state.availableSettle)`, so other subaccounts with positive PnL in the affected product can never settle the locked amount. [8](#0-7) 

2. **Unbacked loss creation** — The artificially enlarged negative `vQuoteBalance` causes `cumulativeFundingLongX18` / `cumulativeFundingShortX18` to shift by more than the real loss, permanently extracting value from all other participants' positions.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The precondition `insurance < lastLiquidationFees` at finalization time is reachable in any stressed-market scenario where:
- The insurance fund was near zero before the last liquidation step (common during cascading liquidations), **and**
- At least one other liquidation's `updateQuoteFromInsurance` call drains the small `I_prev` portion before the liquidator submits the finalization transaction.

The liquidator controls the timing of the `productId = type(uint32).max` finalization call and can deliberately delay it until the condition is met.

---

### Recommendation

Add a non-negativity clamp before passing `v.insurance` to `socializeSubaccount`:

```solidity
v.insurance = insurance;
v.insurance -= lastLiquidationFees;
v.insurance = MathHelper.max(v.insurance, 0); // guard against negative insurance
```

Alternatively, add a `require(insurance >= 0)` guard at the top of `socializeSubaccount` in `PerpEngine.sol`.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
Setup:
  insurance = 100
  lastLiquidationFees = 0

Tx 1 — liquidate subaccount A (perp product, amount > 0):
  liquidationFees = 150
  insurance += 150  → insurance = 250
  lastLiquidationFees = 150

Tx 2 — liquidate subaccount B (spot liability, amount < 0):
  updateQuoteFromInsurance drains 200 from insurance
  insurance = 50

Tx 3 — finalize subaccount A (productId = type(uint32).max):
  v.insurance = 50 - 150 = -100   ← NEGATIVE
  perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(A, -100) called
  
  Inside socializeSubaccount, for product P where balance.vQuoteBalance = -500:
    insuranceCover = min(-100, 500) = -100
    insurance     = -100 - (-100) = 0
    balance.vQuoteBalance = -500 + (-100) = -600   ← MORE negative
    state.availableSettle += -100                  ← DECREASES by 100
  
  Socialization then runs on -600 instead of -500:
    extra 100 units of loss spread across all open-interest holders
  
Assert: state.availableSettle decreased — invariant broken.
```
