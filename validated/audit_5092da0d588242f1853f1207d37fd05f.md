### Title
Unbounded Perp Open Interest Exposes NLP Pool to Unlimited Directional Loss - (File: `core/contracts/PerpEngineState.sol`)

---

### Summary

The Nado protocol tracks per-product `openInterest` in `PerpEngineState` but enforces no on-chain cap. `OffchainExchange.matchOrders` applies no global open interest limit before or after updating balances. The NLP pool (`N_ACCOUNT`) is the implicit counterparty to all net perp exposure. A trader (or coordinated set of traders) can accumulate an arbitrarily large directional perp position bounded only by individual subaccount collateral, leaving the NLP pool with unbounded loss exposure to any oracle price move.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause 1 — `PerpEngineState._updateBalance` has no open interest cap.**

`_updateBalance` updates `state.openInterest` by subtracting the old absolute position and adding the new one, but performs no upper-bound check: [1](#0-0) 

There is no `maxOpenInterest` field in `RiskHelper.RiskStore` and no such parameter anywhere in the risk model: [2](#0-1) 

**Root cause 2 — `OffchainExchange.matchOrders` performs no global open interest check.**

After `_updateBalances` is called for both taker and maker, the only post-trade check is `isHealthy`, which in the deployed `OffchainExchange` contract unconditionally returns `true`: [3](#0-2) 

The balance update and health check sequence in `matchOrders`: [4](#0-3) 

No check is made against any per-product open interest ceiling before or after the balance update.

**Root cause 3 — Oracle price is updated without any circuit breaker tied to open interest.**

`Clearinghouse.updatePrice` applies a new oracle price unconditionally to any product: [5](#0-4) 

A large price move applied to an unbounded open interest position directly translates to an unbounded PnL delta against the NLP pool.

---

### Impact Explanation

The NLP pool subaccounts (managed via `mintNlp`/`burnNlp`/`clearNlpPoolPosition`) hold the quote collateral that backs all perp PnL settlements. When aggregate long open interest exceeds aggregate short open interest for a product, the NLP pool is net short. A price increase then causes the NLP pool to owe settled PnL to all long holders proportional to the total net long open interest. [6](#0-5) 

With no on-chain cap on `state.openInterest`, the NLP pool's maximum loss per oracle price update is:

```
loss = openInterest × |priceChange|
```

This is unbounded. A sufficiently large aggregate position can cause losses exceeding the entire NLP pool's capital, corrupting the `availableSettle` accounting and socializing losses across all remaining LPs via `socializeSubaccount`. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The sequencer controls order matching, but there is no on-chain enforcement preventing open interest accumulation. As the protocol scales, the NLP pool's exposure grows without bound. A well-capitalized trader depositing collateral and signing large orders is a normal, unprivileged action. The sequencer is expected to match valid orders. Without an on-chain cap, the protocol relies entirely on off-chain sequencer policy — a policy that is not enforced or verifiable on-chain and can be bypassed if the sequencer is compromised or misconfigured.

---

### Recommendation

1. Add a `maxOpenInterest` field to `RiskHelper.RiskStore`.
2. In `PerpEngineState._updateBalance` (or in `PerpEngine.updateBalance`), after computing the new `state.openInterest`, revert if it exceeds the configured cap for that product.
3. Set per-product caps calibrated to the depth of the underlying market's liquidity, analogous to the Level Finance recommendation.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Attacker calls `Endpoint.depositCollateral` with a large USDC amount, crediting their subaccount.
2. Attacker signs a large long perp order for product `X` (e.g., amount = 10,000 BTC-equivalent).
3. Sequencer calls `OffchainExchange.matchOrders` with the attacker as taker and `N_ACCOUNT` (NLP pool) as maker.
4. `PerpEngineState._updateBalance` increments `state.openInterest` by 10,000 units — no cap check fires.
5. `isHealthy` returns `true` unconditionally; the trade settles.
6. Sequencer submits a `updatePrice` transaction increasing the BTC oracle price by 5%.
7. Attacker calls `settlePnl` and then `withdrawCollateral`, extracting `10,000 × price × 5%` from the NLP pool.
8. The NLP pool's `availableSettle` is reduced by the same amount; if it goes negative, socialization begins, spreading losses to all LPs.
9. No on-chain check at any step prevented the position from being opened at step 4.

                    insurance,

                    state.cumulativeFundingShortX18 -= fundingPerShare;

                _setBalanceAndUpdateBitmap(productId, subaccount, balance);

        }
