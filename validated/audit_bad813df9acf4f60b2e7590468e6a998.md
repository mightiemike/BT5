### Title
Fast Withdrawal Fees Accumulate in `BaseWithdrawPool` but Are Never Forwarded to the Protocol — (`core/contracts/BaseWithdrawPool.sol`)

---

### Summary

`BaseWithdrawPool.submitFastWithdrawal` computes and collects a fee from every fast withdrawal caller, deducting it from the withdrawal amount (or pulling it from the caller directly), and records it in the `fees[productId]` mapping. However, no function in the contract ever reads `fees[productId]` to forward those tokens to the protocol. The fee tokens are permanently locked in the `WithdrawPool` contract, causing a value leak on every fast withdrawal.

---

### Finding Description

In `submitFastWithdrawal` (lines 81–114 of `BaseWithdrawPool.sol`), the fee is computed and collected in one of two ways:

- **Case A** (`sendTo == msg.sender`): `transferAmount` is reduced by `fee`; the fee tokens remain in the contract.
- **Case B** (`sendTo != msg.sender`): `fee` tokens are pulled from `msg.sender` via `safeTransferFrom` into the contract.

In both cases, the fee is credited to the internal accounting ledger:

```solidity
fees[productId] += fee;
```

After this, `handleWithdrawTransfer` sends only the net `transferAmount` to `sendTo`.
