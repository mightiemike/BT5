### Title
`submit_blocks` Internally Calls `run_mainchain_gc` Without Coordinated Pause-Bypass Roles, Silently Blocking Privileged Relayers When Paused — (`contract/src/lib.rs`)

---

### Summary

`submit_blocks` is decorated with `#[pause]` and grants bypass access to accounts holding `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` (or `Role::DAO`). However, it unconditionally calls `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)` internally. `run_mainchain_gc` carries its own independent pause guard — `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]` — which requires a **different** bypass role. When the contract is paused, any caller holding only `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` (but not `UnrestrictedRunGC`) will pass the outer pause check in `submit_blocks` and then panic inside `run_mainchain_gc`. The `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role is rendered completely inoperative during a pause, defeating its stated design purpose.

---

### Finding Description

`submit_blocks` is the primary block-ingestion entry point for relayers: [1](#0-0) 

The function carries `#[pause]` and the `#[trusted_relayer]` macro is configured with `bypass_roles(Role::DAO, Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks)`, meaning accounts with those roles are intended to submit blocks even while the contract is paused. [2](#0-1) 

The role comment explicitly documents this intent: [3](#0-2) 

However, at line 181, `submit_blocks` unconditionally calls `run_mainchain_gc`: [4](#0-3) 

`run_mainchain_gc` carries its own independent pause guard with a **different** bypass role: [5](#0-4) 

Because `near_plugins`' `#[pause]` macro generates a Rust-level wrapper that checks `env::predecessor_account_id()` against the allowed roles, the pause check inside `run_mainchain_gc` executes even on internal (non-cross-contract) calls. The original external caller's identity is still `env::predecessor_account_id()` at that point. A caller holding `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` but not `UnrestrictedRunGC` will pass the outer check and panic at the inner one.

The two bypass roles are never coordinated:

| Function | Pause bypass role |
|---|---|
| `submit_blocks` | `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks`, `DAO` |
| `run_mainchain_gc` | `UnrestrictedRunGC` |

There is no requirement that a relayer granted `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` also holds `UnrestrictedRunGC`, and no code enforces this coupling.

---

### Impact Explanation

When the contract is paused:

1. A relayer account holding `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` calls `submit_blocks`.
2. The outer `#[pause]` check passes (bypass role present).
3. Block headers are processed in the loop.
4. `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)` is called.
5. The inner `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]` check fires; the caller lacks `UnrestrictedRunGC`; the call panics and the entire transaction is reverted.

The net result: **no blocks can be submitted by any account that does not hold both `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` and `UnrestrictedRunGC`**. The light client's canonical chain state (`mainchain_tip_blockhash`, `mainchain_height_to_header`, `mainchain_header_to_height`) cannot be advanced during a pause period, even by privileged relayers. Downstream consumers calling `verify_transaction_inclusion_v2` after the pause is lifted will be working against a stale chain tip, potentially causing valid proofs to be rejected due to insufficient confirmations or missing block hashes. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The contract has a `PauseManager` role and a full `Pausable` plugin, indicating pausing is an expected operational event (e.g., during upgrades or incident response). The `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role exists precisely to keep the relayer running through such events. The desynchronization is triggered the first time any relayer with only `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` calls `submit_blocks` while the contract is paused — a scenario the role was designed to handle. No adversarial input is required; the bug is structural.

---

### Recommendation

Ensure the pause bypass roles are coordinated across the call chain. The simplest fix is to add `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` (and `Role::DAO`) to the `except` clause of `run_mainchain_gc`:

```rust
#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC, Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks, Role::DAO)))]
pub fn run_mainchain_gc(&mut self, batch_size: u64) { ... }
```

Alternatively, document and enforce at the role-grant level that `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` must always be paired with `UnrestrictedRunGC`. The former is safer because it is enforced in code rather than by operational convention.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy the contract and pause it via a `PauseManager` account.
2. Grant a relayer account only `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` (not `UnrestrictedRunGC`).
3. Have the relayer call `submit_blocks` with a valid batch of headers.
4. Observe the transaction panic with the `run_mainchain_gc` pause error, reverting all state changes.
5. Confirm that granting the same relayer `Role::UnrestrictedRunGC` in addition resolves the panic. [7](#0-6) [5](#0-4)
