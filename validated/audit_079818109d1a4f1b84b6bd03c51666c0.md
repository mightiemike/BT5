### Title
Tier Parameters Fetched at Claim Time Enable Retroactive Reward Manipulation — (File: x/tieredrewards/keeper/claim_rewards.go)

---

### Summary

`processEventsAndClaimBonus` reads the **current** tier from state at claim time and applies its parameters retroactively to every historical bonded segment since the position's last checkpoint. Because no snapshot of tier parameters is taken at accrual time, any governance-approved change to a tier's bonus rate is silently applied to all unclaimed historical periods, causing users who claim before vs. after the update to receive materially different rewards for identical bonded durations.

---

### Finding Description

In `processEventsAndClaimBonus`, the tier is loaded from live state immediately before the historical replay loop:

```go
// claim_rewards.go:167
tier, err := k.getTier(ctx, pos.TierId)
```

That same `tier` object is then passed into `computeSegmentBonus` for every past validator event:

```go
// claim_rewards.go:178
bonus := k.computeSegmentBonus(*pos, tier, segmentStart, evt.Timestamp, evt.TokensPerShare)
```

And again for the still-open current segment after the loop:

```go
// claim_rewards.go:211
bonus := k.computeSegmentBonus(*pos, tier, segmentStart, blockTime, currentRate)
```

There is no per-event or per-period snapshot of the tier's bonus rate. The entire historical accrual window — potentially spanning many blocks or months — is repriced using whatever the tier's parameters happen to be at the moment `ClaimTierRewards` is executed.

If governance passes a `MsgUpdateTier` (or equivalent) that changes the tier's bonus rate while positions are actively accruing, the new rate is applied retroactively to all unclaimed segments. Users who claimed before the update received rewards under the old rate; users who claim after receive a different amount for the same historical period.

---

### Impact Explanation

Two users with identical positions locked at the same block and bonded to the same validator for the same duration receive different bonus rewards solely based on whether they called `ClaimTierRewards` before or after a governance-approved tier parameter change:

- **Rate decrease**: late claimers lose accrued bonus that was earned under the original rate — a direct loss of already-accrued rewards from the rewards pool.
- **Rate increase**: early claimers are under-compensated relative to late claimers for the same historical period.

The corrupted value is the **bonus reward amount paid from the module's rewards pool** to each position owner. The inconsistency is not cosmetic: it results in one user receiving fewer tokens from the pool than another user with an economically identical position.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Governance parameter updates are a standard, documented Cosmos SDK flow reachable by any token holder. The `MsgUpdateParams` path is already exercised in the integration tests (`test_tieredrewards_auth.py`). Tier parameters are expected to evolve over the protocol's lifetime (e.g., adjusting bonus incentives). There is no on-chain guard that prevents a tier update while positions hold unclaimed rewards, making this a realistic scenario during normal protocol operation rather than an adversarial edge case.

---

### Recommendation

Snapshot the effective bonus rate at the time of accrual rather than reading it at claim time. Concretely:

1. Store the tier's bonus rate alongside each `ValidatorEvent` entry when the event is recorded, or
2. Persist a `BonusRateAtLock` field on the position at lock time and use that fixed rate throughout the position's lifetime, or
3. Restrict tier parameter updates (via governance) when any position in that tier has a `LastEventSeq` that has not yet been fully replayed (i.e., unclaimed historical events exist).

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice and Bob each call `LockTier` for tier 1 (bonus rate = R) at block B0, delegating to the same bonded validator.
2. Time advances; the validator remains bonded. Neither user claims.
3. At block B1, governance passes a proposal reducing tier 1's bonus rate to R/2. The change is applied to the live tier record.
4. Alice calls `ClaimTierRewards` at B2 > B1. `processEventsAndClaimBonus` fetches the updated tier (rate = R/2) and computes her bonus for the entire period [B0, B2] at R/2.
5. Bob had called `ClaimTierRewards` at B0.5 < B1 (before the governance update). His bonus for [B0, B0.5] was computed at rate R.
6. Alice and Bob held identical positions for overlapping periods but received different per-unit bonus rates. Alice's accrued rewards for [B0, B1] — earned under rate R — are paid out at R/2, permanently reducing her payout relative to what the protocol owed her at accrual time.

The root cause is the unconditional `getTier(ctx, pos.TierId)` call at line 167 of `claim_rewards.go`, with no historical rate snapshot, making every pending claim vulnerable to retroactive repricing by any successful governance proposal that modifies the tier. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2)
