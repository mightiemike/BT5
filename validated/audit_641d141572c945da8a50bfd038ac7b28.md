### Title
Stale Global `lastLiquidationFees` Causes Understated Insurance in `_finalizeSubaccount`, Forcing Excess Socialization Loss onto Participants — (`core/contracts/ClearinghouseLiq.sol`)

---

### Summary

`lastLiquidationFees` is a single global storage variable. It is overwritten by every normal liquidation of **any** subaccount. When `_finalizeSubaccount` is called for subaccount B, it reads this global value to reserve fees before calling `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount`. If a normal liquidation of a different subaccount A occurred after B's last normal liquidation, the reserved amount is A's fees, not B's. This causes B's socialization to receive less insurance than is actually available, spreading more loss to protocol participants than the invariant permits.

---

### Finding Description

`lastLiquidationFees` is declared as a single `int128` in `ClearinghouseStorage`: [1](#0-0) 

It is written unconditionally at the end of every normal (non-finalization) liquidation in `_handleLiquidationPayment`: [2](#0-1) 

The design intent (per the inline comment) is to reserve the fees from the **last liquidation of the subaccount being finalized**, so that those freshly-added fees do not block the socialization check. However, the variable is global — it tracks the last liquidation of **any** subaccount.

In `_finalizeSubaccount`, the reserved amount is subtracted before socialization and added back after: [3](#0-2) 

`lastLiquidationFees` is **never reset** inside `_finalizeSubaccount`. After the function returns `true`, the variable still holds whatever value was set by the most recent normal liquidation.

---

### Impact Explanation

Consider the sequence:

1. Normal liquidation of subaccount B → `lastLiquidationFees = Y`
2. Normal liquidation of subaccount A (larger position) → `lastLiquidationFees = X`, where `X > Y`
3. Finalization of A → uses `insurance − X`, adds X back; `lastLiquidationFees` remains X
4. Finalization of B → uses `insurance − X` (stale), should use `insurance − Y`

Because `X > Y`, B's socialization receives `X − Y` less insurance than is actually available. The shortfall is not lost — it is added back to `insurance` at line 410 — but during the `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount` call the missing `X − Y` is not applied to cover B's negative `vQuoteBalance`. Instead, that deficit is socialized across all open-interest holders via `cumulativeFundingLongX18` / `cumulativeFundingShortX18`: [4](#0-3) 

This is a direct, permanent transfer of value from protocol participants to the insurance fund: participants absorb losses that the insurance fund had the capacity to cover. The invariant "insurance must be fully applied before socialization" is broken.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires two subaccounts simultaneously under maintenance — a common condition during market stress. Liquidations are permissionless; any liquidator can submit a normal liquidation of a high-fee subaccount A immediately before submitting a finalization of subaccount B. The sequencer processes transactions in submission order, so the ordering is fully attacker-controllable. No privileged access, governance capture, or key compromise is required.

---

### Recommendation

Track `lastLiquidationFees` per subaccount rather than globally. Replace the single `int128 internal lastLiquidationFees` with a `mapping(bytes32 => int128) internal lastLiquidationFees` keyed by the liquidatee's subaccount identifier. In `_handleLiquidationPayment`, write `lastLiquidationFees[txn.liquidatee] = v.liquidationFees`. In `_finalizeSubaccount`, read and clear `lastLiquidationFees[txn.liquidatee]` (set it to 0 after use to avoid stale state on any future re-entry).

---

### Proof of Concept

```
State: insurance = 1000, lastLiquidationFees = 0

Step 1 — normal liquidation of subaccount B (small position, fees = 10):
  insurance = 1010, lastLiquidationFees = 10

Step 2 — normal liquidation of subaccount A (large position, fees = 200):
  insurance = 1210, lastLiquidationFees = 200   ← overwrites B's 10

Step 3 — finalize subaccount A (productId = type(uint32).max):
  v.insurance = 1210 − 200 = 1010
  perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(A, 1010) → returns r_A
  v.insurance = r_A + 200
  insurance = r_A + 200
  lastLiquidationFees still = 200

Step 4 — finalize subaccount B (productId = type(uint32).max):
  v.insurance = (r_A + 200) − 200 = r_A   ← should be (r_A + 200) − 10 = r_A + 190
  perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(B, r_A)   ← 190 less insurance than available
  → 190 units of B's deficit are socialized to participants instead of covered by insurance
  v.insurance += 200
  insurance = result + 200
```

The 190-unit shortfall is permanently borne by open-interest holders through the funding accumulator mutation, while the insurance fund retains those 190 units unnecessarily.
