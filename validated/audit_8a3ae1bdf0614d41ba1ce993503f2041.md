### Title
Blacklisted Token Recipient Causes Silent Permanent Fund Lockup in Slow-Mode Withdrawal — (`core/contracts/BaseWithdrawPool.sol`)

### Summary

If a collateral token (e.g., USDC) blacklists a user's withdrawal destination address after a slow-mode `WithdrawCollateral` transaction is submitted but before it is processed, the withdrawal silently fails, the slow-mode slot is permanently consumed, and the user's on-chain balance remains locked in the clearinghouse with no automatic recovery path.

---

### Finding Description

The withdrawal flow in Nado proceeds as follows:

**Step 1 — Clearinghouse.withdrawCollateral** transfers tokens to the `WithdrawPool` and then calls `submitWithdrawal`: [1](#0-0) 

**Step 2 — BaseWithdrawPool.submitWithdrawal** calls `handleWithdrawTransfer`: [2](#0-1) 

**Step 3 — BaseWithdrawPool.handleWithdrawTransfer** performs an unconditional `safeTransfer` to the recipient: [3](#0-2) 

`safeTransfer` is implemented via a low-level `call` that `require`s success: [4](#0-3) 

If `to` is blacklisted by the token, the `call` returns `false` (or reverts), causing `safeTransfer` to revert. This propagates all the way up through `withdrawCollateral` → `processSlowModeTransactionImpl` → `processSlowModeTransaction`.

**Step 4 — The slow-mode executor silently swallows the revert:** [5](#0-4) 

The slow-mode slot is deleted at line 194 (`delete slowModeTxs[_slowModeConfig.txUpTo++]`) *before* the try-call. The deletion is not rolled back on failure because it happens in the outer frame. The user's balance decrement inside `withdrawCollateral` *is* rolled back (it is inside the try-call), so the balance remains intact — but the slow-mode slot is gone.

The 3-day `SLOW_MODE_TX_DELAY` creates a concrete window during which a user can be blacklisted after submission: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A user whose withdrawal destination is blacklisted by the token during the 3-day delay:

1. Loses the slow-mode fee paid at submission time (charged from their quote balance at `submitSlowModeTransactionImpl` line 370).
2. Has their slow-mode slot permanently consumed with no on-chain notification (no event is emitted on silent failure).
3. Cannot withdraw via the `WithdrawCollateral` (V1) path because `sendTo` is always derived from the sender's own address — every future V1 attempt will fail identically.
4. Must discover the failure off-chain and re-submit using `WithdrawCollateralV2` with a non-blacklisted `sendTo`, paying another slow-mode fee and waiting another 3 days.

If the `WithdrawCollateralV2` `sendTo` is also blacklisted (or becomes blacklisted during its own 3-day window), the cycle repeats indefinitely, with the user losing fees on each attempt and their principal remaining locked.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

USDC (Circle) actively maintains a blacklist and has blacklisted hundreds of addresses, including addresses involved in sanctions or exploits. The 3-day slow-mode delay is a realistic window for a blacklisting event to occur between submission and execution. Any user who deposits USDC as collateral and later has their address sanctioned faces this scenario.

---

### Recommendation

In `BaseWithdrawPool.submitWithdrawal`, wrap the `handleWithdrawTransfer` call in a try-catch (or use a low-level call that does not revert on failure). On failure, credit the amount back to the user's on-chain balance or store it in a claimable mapping so the user can redirect funds to a non-blacklisted address without losing their slow-mode slot or fee.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice deposits USDC collateral and holds a balance in the clearinghouse.
2. Alice submits a slow-mode `WithdrawCollateral` (V1) transaction. The slow-mode fee is deducted from her quote balance. The tx is queued with `executableAt = now + 3 days`.
3. During the 3-day window, USDC blacklists Alice's address.
4. After 3 days, the sequencer (or anyone) calls `executeSlowModeTransaction`.
5. `_executeSlowModeTransaction` deletes Alice's slot from `slowModeTxs`, then calls `this.processSlowModeTransaction(...)` inside a try-catch.
6. Inside the try-call: `clearinghouse.withdrawCollateral` → `handleWithdrawTransfer` → `WithdrawPool.submitWithdrawal` → `token.safeTransfer(alice, amount)` → USDC reverts because Alice is blacklisted.
7. The try-catch catches the revert. Alice's balance decrement is rolled back. Alice's slow-mode slot is permanently consumed.
8. Alice's USDC principal remains in the clearinghouse. She has lost her slow-mode fee and must re-submit via V2 with a different address, paying another fee and waiting another 3 days — with no guarantee the new address won't also be blacklisted.

        IERC20Base self,

            abi.encodeWithSelector(IERC20Base.transfer.selector, to, amount)

            ERR_TRANSFER_FAILED
