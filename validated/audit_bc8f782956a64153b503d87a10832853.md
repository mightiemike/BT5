### Title
Permissionless `executeSlowModeTransaction` Can Be Timed to Force Liquidation of Borderline Subaccounts — (`File: core/contracts/Endpoint.sol`)

---

### Summary

`Endpoint.executeSlowModeTransaction` is callable by any address with no access control. It executes the next pending slow-mode transaction (including `WithdrawCollateral`) once its 3-day delay has elapsed. An attacker can time this call to execute a victim's pending withdrawal at the exact moment the victim's health is borderline, pushing them below the maintenance threshold and enabling liquidation that would not otherwise occur.

---

### Finding Description

`executeSlowModeTransaction` is an unrestricted external function:

```solidity
function executeSlowModeTransaction() external {
    SlowModeConfig memory _slowModeConfig = slowModeConfig;
    _executeSlowModeTransaction(_slowModeConfig, false);
    nSubmissions += 1;
    slowModeConfig = _slowModeConfig;
}
``` [1](#0-0) 

It processes the next queued slow-mode transaction in FIFO order, provided `block.timestamp >= txn.executableAt`:

```solidity
require(
    fromSequencer || (txn.executableAt <= block.timestamp),
    ERR_SLOW_TX_TOO_RECENT
);
``` [2](#0-1) 

Slow-mode transactions include `WithdrawCollateral`, which reduces a subaccount's on-chain balance in `SpotEngine`: [3](#0-2) 

The `WithdrawCollateral` slow-mode path calls `clearinghouse.withdrawCollateral`, which reduces the subaccount's quote balance. Health is then recalculated from the updated balance state. If the withdrawal pushes the subaccount below the maintenance threshold, it becomes liquidatable: [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A victim subaccount that submitted a `WithdrawCollateral` slow-mode transaction while healthy may have its health deteriorate over the 3-day delay period due to market moves, funding payments, or accrued borrow interest. Once the delay expires, any external caller can execute the withdrawal at the worst possible moment — when the victim's health is just above the maintenance threshold — pushing it below and enabling liquidation. The victim loses funds via the liquidation discount that they would not have lost had the withdrawal been processed at a neutral time by the sequencer.

The attacker acting as liquidator profits from the liquidation discount applied in `_handleLiquidationPayment`: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The conditions are realistic and observable on-chain:
1. A user submits a `WithdrawCollateral` slow-mode transaction (visible in `slowModeTxs`).
2. Three days pass — the delay is hardcoded and public.
3. Market conditions move the user's health to borderline during that window (common in volatile markets).
4. The attacker calls `executeSlowModeTransaction` at the optimal block, then submits a signed liquidation transaction to the sequencer.

No privileged access is required. The attacker only needs to monitor the public `slowModeTxs` mapping and `slowModeConfig` state.

---

### Recommendation

Restrict `executeSlowModeTransaction` so that only the original transaction submitter (or the sequencer) can execute it, or add a check that executing a withdrawal does not push the subaccount below the maintenance health threshold. Alternatively, mirror the sequencer's behavior by only allowing the sequencer to execute slow-mode transactions that affect collateral balances, reserving the permissionless path for non-balance-affecting transaction types.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Victim calls `submitSlowModeTransaction` with a `WithdrawCollateral` payload. The transaction is queued at index `N` with `executableAt = block.timestamp + 3 days`. [6](#0-5) 

2. Three days pass. Market moves reduce the victim's health to just above the maintenance threshold.

3. Attacker calls `executeSlowModeTransaction()` — no signature or permission required. [1](#0-0) 

4. The withdrawal is processed: `clearinghouse.withdrawCollateral` reduces the victim's quote balance in `SpotEngine`. [3](#0-2) 

5. The victim's health is now below the maintenance threshold (`isUnderMaintenance` returns true). [7](#0-6) 

6. Attacker submits a signed `LiquidateSubaccount` transaction to the sequencer, liquidating the victim at a discount and profiting from `liquidationFees`. [8](#0-7)
