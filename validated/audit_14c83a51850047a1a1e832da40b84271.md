### Title
Previous Builder Owner Cannot Claim Accumulated `collectedBuilderFee` After Ownership Transfer — (`core/contracts/OffchainExchange.sol`)

---

### Summary

`claimBuilderFee` validates the caller against `builders[builderId].owner`, which always reflects the **current** owner. When `updateBuilder` replaces the owner, the previous owner permanently loses the ability to claim fees accumulated during their tenure. Those fees become claimable only by the new owner.

---

### Finding Description

`collectedBuilderFee[productId][builderId]` accumulates trading fees keyed by `builderId`, not by owner address. [1](#0-0) 

`updateBuilder` performs a full struct replacement, overwriting `builders[builderId].owner` with the new owner address atomically, with no forced fee settlement for the outgoing owner beforehand. [2](#0-1) 

`claimBuilderFee` then gates the entire claim on the **current** owner check:

```solidity
require(
    builders[builderId].owner == address(uint160(bytes20(sender))),
    ERR_UNAUTHORIZED
);
``` [3](#0-2) 

After ownership is transferred, the previous owner's `ClaimBuilderFee` transaction will always revert at this check, even though `collectedBuilderFee[productId][builderId]` still holds fees they earned. The new owner can immediately claim all of it, including fees earned before they took ownership. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

The previous builder owner permanently loses all unclaimed fees accumulated during their tenure. Those fees are not zeroed out — they remain in `collectedBuilderFee[productId][builderId]` and are fully claimable by the new owner. This constitutes a direct, concrete asset loss for the previous owner and an unearned windfall for the new owner. The corrupted state is `collectedBuilderFee[productId][builderId]`: it holds value that the previous owner can no longer access.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Builder ownership changes are a legitimate, documented protocol operation exposed via `ContractOwner.updateBuilders`. [5](#0-4)  The previous builder owner has no on-chain mechanism to force a fee claim before the ownership change is processed, and no way to know when the admin will submit the transaction. Any builder ownership rotation — for key management, business transfer, or protocol maintenance — silently strips the outgoing owner of their earned fees.

---

### Recommendation

Before overwriting `builders[builderId].owner` in `updateBuilder`, sweep all pending `collectedBuilderFee[productId][builderId]` balances to the **current** (outgoing) owner's subaccount. Alternatively, key the fee accumulator by `(builderId, ownerAddress)` so that each owner's earned fees remain independently claimable after an ownership change.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Builder A is registered: `builders[1].owner = addressA`. Trades execute and `collectedBuilderFee[quoteId][1] = 500e6`.
2. Protocol owner calls `ContractOwner.updateBuilders([1], [addressB], ...)`. [6](#0-5)  This enqueues an `UpdateBuilder` slow-mode transaction.
3. Sequencer processes it → `EndpointTx` dispatches to `OffchainExchange.updateBuilder` → `builders[1].owner` is now `addressB`. [7](#0-6) 
4. Owner A submits a `ClaimBuilderFee{sender: subaccountA, builderId: 1}` transaction. `claimBuilderFee` checks `builders[1].owner == addressA` → **false** → `ERR_UNAUTHORIZED`. The 500e6 in fees is permanently inaccessible to A. [8](#0-7) 
5. Owner B submits `ClaimBuilderFee{sender: subaccountB, builderId: 1}`. Check passes. B receives the 500e6 that A earned. [9](#0-8)

    function updateBuilders(
        uint32[] calldata builderIds,
        address[] calldata owners,
        uint32[] calldata defaultFeeTiers,
        int128[] calldata lowestFeeRates,
        int128[] calldata highestFeeRates

            );

                IEndpoint.TransactionType.UpdateBuilder,
