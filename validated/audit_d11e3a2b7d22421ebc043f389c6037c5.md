### Title
Perp Liquidation Deadlock: `sizeIncrement` Constraint and `ERR_LIQUIDATED_TOO_MUCH` Check Create Unliquidatable Positions â€” (`File: core/contracts/ClearinghouseLiq.sol`)

---

### Summary

In `ClearinghouseLiq.sol`, perp liquidation amounts must be exact multiples of `perpSizeIncrement`, and a post-liquidation check rejects any amount that brings the liquidatee's initial health above zero. When the health gap between "maintenance health = 0" and "initial health = 0" is smaller than the health contribution of one `perpSizeIncrement`, no valid liquidation amount exists. The subaccount becomes permanently unliquidatable, accruing bad debt socialized across all participants.

---

### Finding Description

Two independent constraints govern perp liquidations in `liquidateSubaccountImpl`:

**Constraint 1 â€” Size granularity** (`_assertLiquidationAmount`, lines 182â€“186):

```solidity
require(
    txn.amount % perpSizeIncrement == 0,
    ERR_INVALID_LIQUIDATION_AMOUNT
);
maxLiquidatable = perpAmount;
```

Every perp liquidation amount must be a multiple of `perpSizeIncrement`. [1](#0-0) 

**Constraint 2 â€” No over-liquidation** (`_handleLiquidationPayment`, line 573):

```solidity
require(!isAboveInitial(txn.liquidatee), ERR_LIQUIDATED_TOO_MUCH);
```

After the position transfer, the liquidatee's initial health must remain â‰¤ 0. [2](#0-1) 

**The deadlock** arises when, for some integer `k`:

| Amount | Maintenance health after | Initial health after | Accepted? |
|---|---|---|---|
| `k Ă— S` | still < 0 (not enough) | < 0 | âś“ but account still unhealthy |
| `(k+1) Ă— S` | â‰Ą 0 (sufficient) | **> 0** | âś— `ERR_LIQUIDATED_TOO_MUCH` |

No valid multiple of `perpSizeIncrement` exists that simultaneously brings the account out of maintenance health without overshooting initial health. The liquidator is stuck: too little does not heal the account, too much is rejected.

The `_finalizeSubaccount` escape path (triggered by `txn.productId == type(uint32).max`) cannot help either, because it requires all perp positions to already be zero before it proceeds:

```solidity
require(balance.amount == 0, ERR_NOT_FINALIZABLE_SUBACCOUNT);
``` [3](#0-2) 

Since the perp position cannot be reduced to zero through the normal liquidation path, `_finalizeSubaccount` is also blocked.

---

### Impact Explanation

A subaccount holding a perp position in the deadlock zone cannot be liquidated by any liquidator. Its negative maintenance health persists indefinitely. When the position eventually reaches insolvency, `socializeSubaccount` spreads the loss across all open-interest holders via `cumulativeFundingLongX18` / `cumulativeFundingShortX18` adjustments, directly reducing the PnL of every other participant in that market. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The condition is triggered when the health gap between the maintenance and initial thresholds is smaller than the health improvement delivered by one `perpSizeIncrement`. This is more likely for:

- Products configured with a large `sizeIncrement` (e.g., BTC-PERP with `sizeIncrement = 0.001 BTC` at high prices).
- Accounts near the boundary where maintenance and initial health are close together (small spread between `longWeightMaintenance` and `longWeightInitial`).
- Accounts with large unrealized losses that compress the effective health gap.

No privileged access is required. Any liquidator calling `liquidateSubaccountImpl` via `Endpoint.sol` will encounter the revert. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Recommendation

In `_handleLiquidationPayment`, relax the `ERR_LIQUIDATED_TOO_MUCH` check to permit the liquidation amount that is the smallest valid multiple of `perpSizeIncrement` sufficient to bring the account out of maintenance health, even if it incidentally pushes initial health slightly above zero. Concretely, the check should allow the liquidatee's initial health to be positive **only when** the liquidated amount equals the smallest multiple of `perpSizeIncrement` that clears maintenance health and no smaller valid multiple exists. Alternatively, the protocol can allow the liquidator to liquidate the entire remaining position (ignoring the `ERR_LIQUIDATED_TOO_MUCH` guard) when the account is in the deadlock zone, consistent with how Notional's fix permits full liquidation when partial liquidation is structurally impossible. [2](#0-1)
