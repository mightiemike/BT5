### Title
`calculate_next_l2_gas_price_for_fin` and `calculate_next_base_gas_price` apply `VersionedConstants::latest_constants()` `gas_target` and `gas_price_max_change_denominator` to the previous block's gas usage at every version-upgrade boundary, producing a wrong committed L2 gas price — (`File: crates/apollo_consensus_orchestrator/src/fee_market/mod.rs`)

---

### Summary

The EIP-1559 L2 gas price adjustment reads `gas_target` and `gas_price_max_change_denominator` unconditionally from `VersionedConstants::latest_constants()`. When the binary is upgraded to a new Starknet version, the very first call to `calculate_next_l2_gas_price_for_fin` evaluates the *previous* block's gas usage against the *new* version's `gas_target`. Because `gas_target` changed substantially across versions (e.g., 4 000 000 000 → 1 500 000 000 between V0_14_1 and V0_14_2), the price adjustment direction and magnitude are both wrong for the transition block. The resulting `next_l2_gas_price` is committed to the block header and used to charge fees in the following block.

---

### Finding Description

`calculate_next_l2_gas_price_for_fin` fetches `gas_target` from `latest_constants()`: [1](#0-0) 

`calculate_next_base_gas_price` then fetches `gas_price_max_change_denominator` (and validates `gas_target < max_block_size`) from the same `latest_constants()`: [2](#0-1) 

The orchestrator versioned constants show that `gas_target` and `max_block_size` changed materially across every version:

| Version | `gas_target` | `max_block_size` |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| V0_14_0 | 3 200 000 000 | 4 000 000 000 |
| V0_14_1 | 4 000 000 000 | 5 000 000 000 |
| V0_14_2 | 1 500 000 000 | 5 800 000 000 |
| V0_14_3 | 1 040 000 000 | 5 800 000 000 | [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) 

Every block is stamped `StarknetVersion::LATEST`: [5](#0-4) 

So when the binary upgrades from V0_14_1 to V0_14_2, the first `finalize_decision` call invokes `update_l2_gas_price` with the previous block's `l2_gas_used` (which was bounded by the old `max_block_size` = 5 000 000 000), but the formula now uses `gas_target = 1 500 000 000`: [6](#0-5) 

The EIP-1559 formula is:

```
price_change = price × |gas_used − gas_target| / (gas_target × gas_price_max_change_denominator)
```

With the old `gas_target = 4 000 000 000` and `gas_used = 4 000 000 000` (block at target), the expected `price_change = 0`. With the new `gas_target = 1 500 000 000`, the formula sees `gas_delta = 2 500 000 000`, yielding `price_change ≈ +3.47 %` — a price increase when the price should be flat. Worse, a block that was *below* the old target (e.g., `gas_used = 2 500 000 000`) can appear *above* the new target, reversing the direction of adjustment entirely.

The wrong price is then committed via `finalize_decision`: [7](#0-6) 

and forwarded to the cende blob as `next_l2_gas_price`: [8](#0-7) 

---

### Impact Explanation

The wrong `next_l2_gas_price` is written into the committed block header and used to charge fees for every transaction in the following block. At the V0_14_1 → V0_14_2 boundary, a block at the old target produces a spurious ~3.47 % price increase; a block below the old target can produce a price increase instead of a decrease. This is an incorrect fee/gas accounting effect with direct economic impact on users of the transition block.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The bug fires automatically at every binary upgrade that changes `gas_target` in the orchestrator versioned constants. No attacker action is required; the trigger is the normal protocol upgrade event. All four version transitions between V0_14_0 and V0_14_4 changed `gas_target`, so the bug has already been present at every upgrade since V0_14_0.

---

### Recommendation

Pass the Starknet version of the block being finalized into `calculate_next_l2_gas_price_for_fin` and `calculate_next_base_gas_price`, and resolve `gas_target`, `max_block_size`, and `gas_price_max_change_denominator` from `VersionedConstants::get(version)` rather than `latest_constants()`. The version is already available in `ProposalInit::starknet_version` at the call site in `finalize_decision`.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Scenario: V0_14_1 → V0_14_2 upgrade**

1. Last block under V0_14_1: `gas_used = 4 000 000 000` (exactly at the V0_14_1 `gas_target`).
2. Binary upgrades; `latest_constants()` now returns V0_14_2 constants.
3. `calculate_next_l2_gas_price_for_fin` reads `gas_target = 1 500 000 000` (V0_14_2).
4. `gas_delta = |4 000 000 000 − 1 500 000 000| = 2 500 000 000`.
5. `denominator = 1 500 000 000 × 48 = 72 000 000 000`.
6. `price_change = price × 2 500 000 000 / 72 000 000 000 ≈ price × 3.47 %`.
7. **Expected**: `price_change = 0` (block was at target). **Actual**: price rises ~3.47 %.

**Direction-reversal sub-case:**

1. Last V0_14_1 block: `gas_used = 2 500 000 000` (below old target of 4 000 000 000).
2. Expected: price decreases by `price × 1 500 000 000 / (4 000 000 000 × 48) ≈ −0.78 %`.
3. Actual (new `gas_target = 1 500 000 000`): `gas_used > gas_target`, so price *increases* by `price × 1 000 000 000 / (1 500 000 000 × 48) ≈ +1.39 %`.

The sign of the adjustment is inverted, and the committed `next_l2_gas_price` is wrong by approximately 2.17 % of the current price.
