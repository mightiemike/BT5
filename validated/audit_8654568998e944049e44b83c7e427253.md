### Title
Liquidation Can Worsen Subaccount Maintenance Health When `longWeightMaintenanceX18 > 1 - MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18` — (`core/contracts/ClearinghouseStorage.sol`)

---

### Summary

The liquidation penalty formula in `getLiqPriceX18` enforces a minimum discount of `MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18 = 0.5%` to guarantee liquidator incentive. However, when a product's `longWeightMaintenanceX18` exceeds `0.995` (i.e., `1 - 0.005`), this minimum penalty is larger than the weight-based discount, causing each liquidation step to make the liquidatee's maintenance health **more negative** rather than less. The protocol's only post-liquidation guard checks that initial health does not go above zero — it does not verify that maintenance health improved. This enables cascading liquidations that progressively drain the victim's subaccount.

---

### Finding Description

In `ClearinghouseStorage.getLiqPriceX18`, the liquidation price for a long position is computed as:

```solidity
int128 penaltyX18 = (RiskHelper._getWeightX18(risk, amount, IProductEngine.HealthType.MAINTENANCE) - ONE) / 5;
if (penaltyX18.abs() < MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18) {
    penaltyX18 = -MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18; // floor at -0.5%
}
return (risk.priceX18.mul(ONE + penaltyX18), risk.priceX18);
``` [1](#0-0) 

For a long position with maintenance weight `w_m`, the natural penalty is `(w_m - 1) / 5`. When `w_m` is close to 1 (e.g., `0.998`), the natural penalty is tiny (`-0.04%`), so the minimum floor of `-0.5%` is applied instead.

The net maintenance health change from liquidating amount `a` at price `p` is:

```
Δhealth = a * p * (1 + penalty) - a * p * w_m
        = a * p * (1 - 0.005 - w_m)
```

When `w_m > 0.995`, this expression is **negative** — health worsens after liquidation.

The only post-liquidation health check is:

```solidity
require(!isAboveInitial(txn.liquidatee), ERR_LIQUIDATED_TOO_MUCH);
``` [2](#0-1) 

This guard only prevents over-liquidation (initial health going above zero). It does **not** verify that maintenance health improved. A health-worsening liquidation passes this check because the subaccount remains deeply underwater on both initial and maintenance health.

There is no parameter validation at product registration time that enforces `longWeightMaintenanceX18 <= 1 - MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18`. [3](#0-2) 

---

### Impact Explanation

For any product with `longWeightMaintenanceX18 > 0.995` (e.g., a stablecoin pair or low-risk asset configured with a 99.8% maintenance weight):

1. A subaccount with maintenance health just below zero becomes eligible for liquidation.
2. A liquidator submits a partial liquidation; the liquidatee receives quote at a 0.5% discount from oracle price.
3. Because `w_m = 0.998 > 0.995`, the health contribution lost from the spot position exceeds the quote received, making maintenance health **more negative**.
4. The subaccount remains eligible for liquidation (maintenance health still < 0) and the `!isAboveInitial` check still passes.
5. The liquidator repeats, extracting a 0.5% discount on each step while the victim's net position deteriorates.

**Concrete example:**
- Product X: price = $100, `w_m = 0.998`, `w_i = 0.95`
- Subaccount: 100 units Product X, −$9,990 quote
- Maintenance health before: `100 × 100 × 0.998 − 9990 = −$10` (eligible)
- Liquidate 10 units at $99.50 (min penalty): liquidatee gains $995, loses 10 units
- Maintenance health after: `90 × 100 × 0.998 − 8995 = −$13` (worse)
- Each step worsens health by $3; liquidator profits $5 per step

The liquidatee loses collateral faster than their debt is reduced, enabling full account drainage through repeated liquidations.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The condition `longWeightMaintenanceX18 > 0.995` is realistic for:
- Stablecoin assets (e.g., USDC, USDT, DAI) where near-unity weights are appropriate
- Any low-volatility asset where a protocol operator sets a high maintenance weight to allow efficient capital use

No special privilege is required to trigger the cascade once the product is listed. Any user whose subaccount holds such an asset and becomes marginally unhealthy is vulnerable. The liquidator is an unprivileged subaccount — the only restriction is `!isIsolatedSubaccount(txn.sender)` and `txn.sender != txn.liquidatee`. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Recommendation

**Option 1 — Validate at parameter-setting time.** When adding or updating a product's risk parameters, enforce:

```solidity
require(
    longWeightMaintenanceX18 <= ONE - MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18,
    "Weight incompatible with min liquidation penalty"
);
```

**Option 2 — Post-liquidation invariant check.** After `_handleLiquidationPayment`, assert that the liquidatee's maintenance health did not decrease:

```solidity
int128 healthBefore = getHealthFromClearinghouse(txn.liquidatee, MAINTENANCE);
_handleLiquidationPayment(...);
int128 healthAfter = getHealthFromClearinghouse(txn.liquidatee, MAINTENANCE);
require(healthAfter >= healthBefore, "Liquidation worsened health");
```

**Option 3 — Adjust the minimum penalty formula.** Instead of a fixed floor, compute the minimum penalty as `max(MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18, ONE - w_m)` so the discount always exceeds the weight gap.

### Proof of Concept

```
Setup:
  - Product X: longWeightMaintenanceX18 = 0.998e18, longWeightInitialX18 = 0.95e18, price = 100e18
  - Subaccount A: +100 units Product X, -9990e18 quote

Step 0 (initial state):
  maintenance health = 100 * 100 * 0.998 - 9990 = -10   [eligible: health < 0]
  initial health     = 100 * 100 * 0.950 - 9990 = -490  [initial also < 0]

Step 1 — liquidate 10 units:
  penaltyX18 = (0.998e18 - 1e18) / 5 = -0.0004e18  → floored to -0.005e18
  liquidation price = 100 * (1 - 0.005) = 99.50
  liquidatee: -10 units Product X, +995 quote

  maintenance health = 90 * 100 * 0.998 - (9990 - 995) = 8982 - 8995 = -13  [WORSE]
  initial health     = 90 * 100 * 0.950 - 8995 = 8550 - 8995 = -445          [still < 0]

  → !isAboveInitial check passes (initial health = -445 < 0)
  → isUnderMaintenance check passes for next liquidation (maintenance health = -13 < 0)

Step 2 — liquidate another 10 units:
  maintenance health = 80 * 100 * 0.998 - (8995 - 995) = 7984 - 8000 = -16  [WORSE again]

Each step: health worsens by ~3, liquidator profits $5 (buys $1000 of asset for $995).
Cascade continues until subaccount is fully drained.
```
