### Title
`registerMerkleRoot()` Does Not Validate Contract Balance Against Total Distribution Amount — (`File: core/contracts/Airdrop.sol`)

---

### Summary

`Airdrop.registerMerkleRoot()` accepts a Merkle root encoding per-user reward allocations without verifying that the contract holds sufficient token balance to cover the aggregate of all claims in that root. If the contract is underfunded at registration time, early claimants drain the available balance and later claimants with equally valid proofs receive nothing.

---

### Finding Description

`registerMerkleRoot()` stores a Merkle root for a given week with no balance check: [1](#0-0) 

The root encodes `(address, totalAmount)` leaves for every eligible user. The total of all `totalAmount` values across all leaves represents the full liability the contract must honor for that week. No on-chain check enforces that `IERC20(token).balanceOf(address(this))` is at least equal to this aggregate before the root is committed.

When a user calls `claim()`, `_claim()` immediately attempts a live token transfer: [2](#0-1) 

`SafeERC20.safeTransfer` will revert if the contract balance is insufficient. Because `claimed[week][sender]` is written *before* the transfer (line 62), a revert on transfer leaves the state partially updated — but more critically, if the contract is simply underfunded, the first users to claim succeed and the remaining balance is exhausted, causing all subsequent valid claimants to revert permanently for that week. [3](#0-2) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Users holding valid Merkle proofs for a given week are unable to claim their allocated rewards. Their `claim()` calls revert with an ERC-20 transfer failure. The `claimed` mapping is never written for them (the revert unwinds state), so they are not marked as having claimed, but the tokens they are owed do not exist in the contract. The reward liability is permanently unserviceable for those users unless the contract is manually topped up — an action not enforced or guaranteed by any on-chain mechanism.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The protocol's own acknowledgement in the external report confirms the off-chain accounting path: the owner fetches the contract balance before generating the Merkle tree. Any race condition, off-chain accounting error, delayed token transfer, or multi-week accumulation mismatch can result in the contract being underfunded at the moment `registerMerkleRoot()` is called. Because the contract provides no on-chain guard, the invariant is enforced entirely by off-chain discipline, making this a realistic operational failure path.

---

### Recommendation

Add a balance sufficiency check inside `registerMerkleRoot()`. Since the Merkle root is opaque on-chain, the simplest approach is to require the caller to supply the total distribution amount for the week alongside the root, and verify the contract holds at least that amount:

```solidity
function registerMerkleRoot(uint32 week, bytes32 merkleRoot, uint256 totalDistribution)
    external
    onlyOwner
{
    pastWeeks += 1;
    require(week == pastWeeks, "Invalid week provided.");
    require(
        IERC20(token).balanceOf(address(this)) >= totalDistribution,
        "Insufficient balance for distribution."
    );
    merkleRoots[week] = merkleRoot;
}
```

This mirrors the fix implied by the external report: the `_amount` parameter should be validated against the actual contract balance before committing the root. The off-chain generator must ensure `totalDistribution` equals the sum of all leaf amounts in the tree.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Owner generates a Merkle tree for week 1 with two users: Alice (500 tokens) and Bob (500 tokens), total = 1000 tokens.
2. Owner calls `registerMerkleRoot(1, root)` when the contract holds only 600 tokens. No revert occurs.
3. Alice calls `claim([{week:1, totalAmount:500, proof:...}])`. Transfer of 500 tokens succeeds. Contract balance: 100 tokens.
4. Bob calls `claim([{week:1, totalAmount:500, proof:...}])`. `_verifyProof` passes (valid proof, not yet claimed). `SafeERC20.safeTransfer` reverts — only 100 tokens remain.
5. Bob's valid claim is permanently unserviceable unless the contract is manually refunded. Bob has no on-chain recourse. [1](#0-0) [4](#0-3)
