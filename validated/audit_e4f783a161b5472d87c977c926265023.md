### Title
`burnNlp` Uses Weaker `MAINTENANCE` Health Check While All Peer Collateral Operations Enforce `INITIAL` — (`core/contracts/Clearinghouse.sol`)

---

### Summary

`burnNlp` enforces only a `MAINTENANCE`-level health check after reducing a subaccount's collateral, while every other collateral-modifying operation in the same contract (`mintNlp`, `withdrawCollateral`, `transferQuote`, `nlpProfitShare`) enforces the stricter `INITIAL`-level check. This inconsistency is a direct analog to Velodrome Issue 5.3.9: one path in a family of related functions omits a protection that all sibling paths apply, allowing a caller to reach a protocol state that those siblings explicitly forbid.

---

### Finding Description

Four collateral-modifying functions in `Clearinghouse.sol` all gate their final state on `INITIAL` health:

- `mintNlp` — [1](#0-0) 
- `withdrawCollateral` — [2](#0-1) 
- `transferQuote` — [3](#0-2) 
- `nlpProfitShare` — [4](#0-3) 

`burnNlp` is the sole exception. After debiting the sender's NLP balance and crediting quote, it checks only `MAINTENANCE` health: [5](#0-4) 

The in-code comment acknowledges the weaker check is deliberate ("Burning NLP can decrease health if the burn fee exceeds the health improvement from the withdrawal"), but the consequence is that a caller can exit `burnNlp` with `INITIAL` health strictly below zero while `MAINTENANCE` health remains non-negative. No other collateral exit path permits this outcome.

The burn fee is `max(1e18, quoteAmount / 1000)` — at minimum 1 unit of quote, at most 0.1 % of the redeemed value: [6](#0-5) 

A subaccount positioned exactly at the `INITIAL` health boundary before the burn will cross into negative `INITIAL` health by the fee amount, while remaining above the `MAINTENANCE` threshold (maintenance weights are strictly less conservative than initial weights per `RiskHelper.RiskStore`): [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A subaccount that exits `burnNlp` with negative `INITIAL` health and positive `MAINTENANCE` health is in a state that:

1. **Cannot be liquidated** — `liquidateSubaccountImpl` requires `isUnderMaintenance` (maintenance health < 0) as its entry condition. [8](#0-7) 

2. **Can still have orders matched** — `matchOrders` calls `isHealthy`, which unconditionally returns `true` in `OffchainExchange`. [9](#0-8) 

3. **Is blocked from all other collateral exits** — `withdrawCollateral`, `transferQuote`, and `mintNlp` all require `INITIAL` health ≥ 0, so the subaccount cannot improve its position through those paths.

The net result is a subaccount that holds open perp or spot positions below the protocol's initial margin requirement, is immune to liquidation, and can continue to accumulate fills. This violates the invariant that any subaccount with negative `INITIAL` health must either be liquidatable or unable to trade. The protocol's insurance fund and socialization mechanism are exposed to a larger-than-intended loss if the position subsequently deteriorates past the maintenance threshold.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The trigger condition is realistic: a user who mints NLP while near the `INITIAL` health boundary, then burns it, crosses the boundary by the burn fee. The fee is small (≥ 0.1 % of redeemed value), so the required proximity to the boundary is achievable through normal position management. The `BurnNlp` transaction type is a standard user-facing operation submitted through the `Endpoint` slow-mode or sequencer path, requiring no privileged access.
