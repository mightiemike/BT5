### Title
Fees and Funding Accumulate Over Full Sequencer Downtime Without Exclusion Mechanism — (`core/contracts/EndpointTx.sol`)

---

### Summary

The `SpotTick` and `PerpTick` transaction handlers in `EndpointTx.sol` compute `dt` as the raw difference between the sequencer-supplied `txn.time` and the last recorded engine time. When the sequencer resumes after any downtime period, it submits the current oracle time, causing the full offline interval to be included in `dt`. Both `spotEngine.updateStates(dt)` and `perpEngine.updateStates(dt, ...)` then apply interest and funding for the entire elapsed period — including time during which users were unable to modify their positions.

---

### Finding Description

In `EndpointTx.processTransactionImpl()`, the `SpotTick` and `PerpTick` branches compute `dt` as:

```solidity
uint128 dt = t.spotTime == 0 ? 0 : txn.time - t.spotTime;
spotEngine.updateStates(dt);
t.spotTime = txn.time;
```

and

```solidity
uint128 dt = t.perpTime == 0 ? 0 : txn.time - t.perpTime;
perpEngine.updateStates(dt, txn.avgPriceDiffs);
t.perpTime = txn.time;
``` [1](#0-0) 

The value `txn.time` is entirely sequencer-supplied and is not validated against `block.timestamp`. The only on-chain guard is a maximum-interval check inside each engine:

```solidity
require(dt < 7 * SECONDS_PER_DAY, ERR_INVALID_TIME);
``` [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) 

This cap allows up to 7 days of accumulated fees in a single tick. Any downtime shorter than 7 days passes through unchecked.

**Spot interest path:** `SpotEngineState._updateState()` compounds the borrow rate over the full `dt`:

```solidity
borrowRateMultiplierX18 = (ONE + borrowerRateX18).pow(int128(dt));
``` [4](#0-3) 

The resulting multiplier is applied to `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` and `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`, permanently increasing all borrowers' normalized debt for the entire downtime window. [5](#0-4) 

**Perp funding path:** `PerpEngineState.updateStates()` applies funding linearly over `dt`:

```solidity
int128 paymentAmount = priceDiffX18.mul(dtX18).div(ONE_DAY_X18);
state.cumulativeFundingLongX18 += paymentAmount;
state.cumulativeFundingShortX18 += paymentAmount;
``` [6](#0-5) 

This permanently shifts the cumulative funding accumulators by the full downtime interval, which is then settled against every open perp balance via `_updateBalance()`. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

During sequencer downtime, all user-facing operations (order matching, withdrawals, position modifications) are gated behind the sequencer and are unavailable. When the sequencer resumes and submits a `SpotTick` or `PerpTick` with the actual elapsed oracle time, the full downtime is billed as productive fee-accruing time:

- Spot borrowers' effective debt increases by the compounded borrow rate over the downtime.
- Perp position holders pay or receive funding for the entire downtime, which they had no ability to hedge or close against.
- Users who would have closed or reduced positions during the downtime are charged fees they could not avoid.

The corrupted state variables are `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18`, `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` (spot), and `cumulativeFundingLongX18` / `cumulativeFundingShortX18` (perp) — all of which feed directly into health checks and liquidation thresholds. [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Sequencer downtime is a realistic operational event (planned maintenance, infrastructure failure, network partition). Any downtime under 7 days results in the full interval being charged as fees. No on-chain mechanism exists to distinguish intentional downtime from normal operation. The sequencer resuming with the current oracle time is the expected, non-malicious behavior that triggers the issue.

---

### Recommendation

Introduce a per-tick `dt` cap well below 7 days (e.g., 1–4 hours) so that extended downtime cannot be billed as a single large fee interval. Alternatively, track a `lastTickTime` against `block.timestamp` on-chain and reject or clamp `txn.time` values that imply implausibly large gaps. This mirrors the recommendation in the referenced report: exclude the downtime window from fee-generating intervals.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Users open spot borrow positions and perp positions.
2. The sequencer goes offline for 72 hours (3 days < 7-day cap).
3. Users cannot submit any transactions — all paths require sequencer processing.
4. Sequencer resumes and submits `SpotTick` with `txn.time = lastSpotTime + 259200`.
5. `dt = 259200` passes the `< 7 * SECONDS_PER_DAY` check.
6. `spotEngine.updateStates(259200)` compounds 3 days of borrow interest into `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18`.
7. Sequencer submits `PerpTick` with `txn.time = lastPerpTime + 259200`.
8. `perpEngine.updateStates(259200, avgPriceDiffs)` applies 3 days of funding to `cumulativeFundingLongX18` / `cumulativeFundingShortX18`.
9. All open positions now reflect 3 days of fees that accrued while users had zero ability to act. [8](#0-7) [9](#0-8) [10](#0-9)
