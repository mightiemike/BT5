### Title
Accrued Bonus Rewards Permanently Lost When Bonus Pool Is Insufficient During Redelegation Slash — (`x/tieredrewards/keeper/slash.go`, `x/tieredrewards/keeper/claim_rewards.go`)

---

### Summary

`slashRedelegationPosition` calls `processEventsAndClaimBonus`, which performs irreversible on-chain state mutations (event ref-count decrements and checkpoint advancement) **before** checking pool sufficiency. When `ErrInsufficientBonusPool` is returned, `slashRedelegationPosition` silently continues and persists the advanced position state. The position owner permanently loses all accrued bonus for the pre-slash period.

---

### Finding Description

`processEventsAndClaimBonus` processes events in a loop: [1](#0-0) 

For every event, it calls `pos.UpdateLastEventSeq(entry.Seq)` (in-memory) and `k.decrementEventRefCount(ctx, valAddr, entry.Seq)` (committed KV-store write). After the loop it calls `applyBonusAccrualCheckpoint` advancing `LastBonusAccrual` in memory: [2](#0-1) 

Only **after** all of this does it check pool balance: [3](#0-2) 

When the pool check fails, `ErrInsufficientBonusPool` is returned — but the ref-count decrements are already committed to the KV store and the in-memory `pos` has `LastEventSeq` and `LastBonusAccrual` fully advanced.

`slashRedelegationPosition` catches this error, logs it, and falls through: [4](#0-3) 

It then calls `setPositionWithState` with the already-advanced `pos`, persisting the consumed checkpoint: [5](#0-4) 

The invariant — *accrued bonus is either paid or preserved for future claim* — is broken. The events are gone (ref counts at zero, entries deleted), `LastBonusAccrual` is advanced to `blockTime`, and no coins were transferred.

---

### Impact Explanation

The position owner permanently loses all bonus rewards accrued from `LastBonusAccrual` up to the slash block. Even after the bonus pool is refilled, a subsequent `ClaimTierRewards` call will find no unprocessed events and a fully advanced `LastBonusAccrual`, so it computes zero bonus for the lost period. This is a direct, unrecoverable economic loss proportional to the position's staked amount, tier `BonusApy`, and the duration of the accrual window.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The bonus pool is a finite module account. Any period of high claim activity, a large single claim, or a governance-controlled pool drain can leave it temporarily empty. Redelegation slashes are a normal chain event (double-sign or downtime on the destination validator during the redelegation lock). The two conditions — drained pool and a redelegation slash — can co-occur naturally without any attacker coordination. A malicious actor can also deliberately drain the pool immediately before a known slash to guarantee the loss.

---

### Recommendation

Move the `sufficientBonusPoolBalance` check **before** any event processing and checkpoint advancement. Compute the total bonus in a read-only pass first, verify pool sufficiency, and only then commit ref-count decrements and advance the checkpoint. Alternatively, if the pool is insufficient during a slash hook, skip both the payment **and** the checkpoint/ref-count advancement so the position retains its accrual state for a future claim.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Create a tier position and redelegate it to validator V.
2. Drain the `RewardsPoolName` module account to zero (e.g., via a series of `ClaimTierRewards` calls from other positions).
3. Slash validator V for a double-sign, triggering `BeforeRedelegationSlashed` → `slashRedelegationPosition` → `processEventsAndClaimBonus`.
4. Observe: `decrementEventRefCount` is called for all pending events (KV writes committed), `LastBonusAccrual` is advanced to the slash block, but `SendCoinsFromModuleToAccount` is never reached.
5. Refill the bonus pool.
6. Call `ClaimTierRewards` for the position.
7. Assert: bonus returned is zero for the pre-slash accrual period, confirming permanent loss.
