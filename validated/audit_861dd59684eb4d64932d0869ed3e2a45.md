### Title
`submit_blocks` Unconditionally Calls `run_mainchain_gc` Which Requires a Separate Privileged Role, Rendering the `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` Bypass Non-Functional During a Pause - (`File: contract/src/lib.rs`)

---

### Summary

`submit_blocks` is designed to be callable by accounts holding `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` even when the contract is paused. However, it unconditionally calls `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)` at line 181, and `run_mainchain_gc` is independently gated by `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]`. Because `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` and `UnrestrictedRunGC` are two distinct roles, a relayer holding only `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` will always have the inner `run_mainchain_gc` call panic when the contract is paused. The `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` bypass is therefore completely non-functional.

---

### Finding Description

`submit_blocks` carries `#[pause]` and `#[trusted_relayer]`. The `trusted_relayer` macro is configured with `bypass_roles(Role::DAO, Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks)`, which allows accounts holding `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` to call `submit_blocks` even while the contract is paused. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

Inside `submit_blocks`, after processing all submitted headers, the function unconditionally calls `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)`: [3](#0-2) 

`run_mainchain_gc` is independently decorated with its own pause guard: [4](#0-3) 

This guard only exempts callers holding `Role::UnrestrictedRunGC`. These are two entirely separate roles in the `Role` enum: [5](#0-4) 

When the contract is paused and a relayer holding only `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` calls `submit_blocks`:

1. The `submit_blocks` pause check is bypassed (the `bypass_roles` in `trusted_relayer` covers it).
2. All submitted headers are processed successfully.
3. `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)` is called at line 181.
4. `run_mainchain_gc`'s own `#[pause]` check fires. `env::predecessor_account_id()` is still the original relayer account, which does not hold `Role::UnrestrictedRunGC`.
5. The call panics and the entire transaction reverts — no headers are stored.

This is structurally identical to the GMX analog: `submit_blocks` calls a sub-function that requires a privileged role the intended caller never holds, making the calling function permanently broken for its intended callers under the exact conditions it was designed to handle.

---

### Impact Explanation

The `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role exists precisely to allow critical relayers to keep the Bitcoin light client synchronized during an emergency pause. Because `submit_blocks` always calls `run_mainchain_gc`, and `run_mainchain_gc` requires a separate role (`UnrestrictedRunGC`) to execute while paused, the bypass is completely inoperative. During any pause, no relayer holding only `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` can submit a single block header. The canonical chain tracked by the contract stops advancing, and any downstream SPV verification that depends on a live chain tip becomes stale or fails.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The trigger requires the contract to be paused by a `PauseManager`. Pausing is an expected operational action (e.g., during an upgrade or incident response). The `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role is explicitly documented as the mechanism to keep relayers operational during such pauses. Any deployment that grants this role and then pauses the contract will immediately encounter the broken behavior. No adversarial input is required — the bug fires on every `submit_blocks` call from an `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` account while paused.

---

### Recommendation

Either:

1. Add `Role::UnrestrictedRunGC` to the `bypass_roles` of the `run_mainchain_gc` pause guard, and ensure that any account granted `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` is also granted `UnrestrictedRunGC`; or
2. Extract the GC logic called from within `submit_blocks` into a private helper that bypasses the pause check, since the pause guard on `run_mainchain_gc` is only meaningful for direct external calls, not for the internal invocation from `submit_blocks`.

Option 2 is cleaner: the `#[pause]` guard on `run_mainchain_gc` is intended to protect the public external entry point. The internal call from `submit_blocks` is already protected by `submit_blocks`'s own pause guard and should not be subject to a second, incompatible role check.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
State: contract is paused by PauseManager.
Actor: relayer account `relayer.near` holds Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks but NOT Role::UnrestrictedRunGC.

Call: relayer.near → submit_blocks([header1, header2, ...])

Execution trace:
  1. #[trusted_relayer] bypass_roles check: UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks present → pass.
  2. #[pause] check on submit_blocks: bypassed by trusted_relayer bypass_roles → pass.
  3. Loop: submit_block_header() called for each header → succeeds (no pause guard here).
  4. self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers) called (lib.rs:181).
  5. #[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))] fires inside run_mainchain_gc (lib.rs:376).
  6. predecessor = relayer.near; does NOT hold UnrestrictedRunGC → PANIC.
  7. Entire transaction reverts. No headers stored. Chain tip unchanged.

Result: UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks is non-functional during any pause.
