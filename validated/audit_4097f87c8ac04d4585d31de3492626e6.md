### Title
Small Positions Create Unliquidatable Bad Debt Due to `MIN_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT` vs. `LIQUIDATION_FEE` Mismatch and Withdrawal Bypass - (File: `core/contracts/common/Constants.sol`, `core/contracts/Clearinghouse.sol`)

---

### Summary

The Nado protocol allows creation of positions too small to be economically liquidated. The flat `LIQUIDATION_FEE = $1` charged to liquidators requires a minimum position notional of ~$400 for profitable liquidation, yet `MIN_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT = $0.1` permits positions far below this threshold. Additionally, even the higher `MIN_FIRST_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT = $5` check for new accounts can be bypassed: `Clearinghouse.withdrawCollateral` only enforces `getHealth >= 0` post-withdrawal with no minimum remaining collateral floor, allowing a user to drain collateral after deposit and leave a tiny, unliquidatable position.

---

### Finding Description

**Root Cause 1 — `MIN_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT` is far below the minimum economically viable liquidation size:**

`MIN_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT = ONE / 10` ($0.1) is the threshold for existing accounts. [1](#0-0) 

The liquidator's gross profit from a liquidation is `50% × penalty × position_value`, where the minimum penalty is `MIN_NON_SPREAD_LIQ_PENALTY_X18 = 0.5%`. [2](#0-1) 

The liquidator also pays a flat `LIQUIDATION_FEE = $1` charged to their protocol account: [3](#0-2) 

This fee is deducted from the liquidator's quote balance via `chargeFee(signedTx.tx.sender, LIQUIDATION_FEE)` before the liquidation executes: [4](#0-3) 

The minimum profitable position size is therefore: `$1 / (50% × 0.5%) = $400`. Any position below ~$400 notional is unprofitable to liquidate. With `MIN_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT = $0.1` and typical leverage (e.g., 5–10×), a user can open a $0.5–$1 notional position — far below the $400 threshold.

**Root Cause 2 — `withdrawCollateral` has no minimum remaining collateral check, bypassing `MIN_FIRST_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT`:**

`withdrawCollateral` in `Clearinghouse.sol` only checks that INITIAL health remains non-negative after the withdrawal: [5](#0-4) 

There is no check on the absolute remaining collateral value. A user who deposited $5 (meeting `MIN_FIRST_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT`) can withdraw $4.90 immediately after opening a position, leaving $0.10 collateral — identical to the WiseLending bypass pattern.

The `LIQUIDATION_FEE_FRACTION = 50%` confirms that only half the penalty accrues to the liquidator; the rest goes to the insurance fund: [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6) [8](#0-7) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Positions below ~$400 notional will not be liquidated because the liquidation profit is less than the $1 fee. These positions accumulate as bad debt. When the insurance fund is exhausted, `socializeSubaccount` is called, which adjusts `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` downward, socializing losses across all depositors: [9](#0-8) 

Repeated exploitation with many small accounts (each costing only $0.1 to set up) can systematically drain the insurance fund and cause protocol-wide losses.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High likelihood of occurrence.** Any existing account holder can deposit $0.1, open a leveraged perp position, and abandon it if it goes underwater. The attack is cheap and repeatable. For new accounts, the $5 minimum deposit is trivially bypassed via the withdrawal path. On Ink Chain (L2), gas costs are negligible, making the $1 `LIQUIDATION_FEE` the sole economic barrier — and it is already priced into the attack cost.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Enforce a minimum position notional on-chain** in `OffchainExchange` or `Clearinghouse` such that no position can be opened or left open below the minimum profitable liquidation size (e.g., $400 notional given current fee parameters), or make `LIQUIDATION_FEE` proportional to position size rather than flat.

2. **Add a minimum remaining collateral check in `withdrawCollateral`** to prevent the deposit-then-withdraw bypass. After withdrawal, verify that the subaccount's total collateral value (not just health) remains above a protocol-defined floor, analogous to enforcing `MIN_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT` on the resulting state.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Path A — Direct small position (existing account):**
1. Existing user calls `Endpoint.depositCollateral` with $0.1 (meets `MIN_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT = $0.1`).
2. Sequencer matches a perp order, opening a $1 notional position (10× leverage; INITIAL health passes).
3. Price moves adversely; MAINTENANCE health < 0.
4. Liquidation gross profit = 50% × 0.5% × $1 = **$0.0025 < $1 `LIQUIDATION_FEE`**.
5. No liquidator submits; bad debt accumulates against the insurance fund.

**Path B — Withdrawal bypass (new account):**
1. New user calls `Endpoint.depositCollateral` with $5 (meets `MIN_FIRST_DEPOSIT_AMOUNT = $5`).
2. Sequencer matches a perp order for $10 notional (2× leverage; INITIAL health = $5 × 0.9 − $10 × 0.1 = $3.5 > 0).
3. User calls `withdrawCollateral` for $3.40; post-withdrawal INITIAL health = $1.60 × 0.9 − $10 × 0.1 = $0.44 ≥ 0 — **check passes**.
4. User now holds $10 notional perp with only $1.60 collateral.
5. Price moves adversely; MAINTENANCE health < 0.
6. Liquidation gross profit = 50% × 0.5% × $10 = **$0.025 < $1 `LIQUIDATION_FEE`**.
7. No liquidation; bad debt accumulates.
