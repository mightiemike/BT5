### Title
Stale Utilization Rate Applied Retroactively Over Full `dt` Window in `_updateState` - (File: `core/contracts/SpotEngineState.sol`)

### Summary

`SpotEngineState._updateState` computes the borrow interest multiplier using the **current** utilization ratio at the moment a `SpotTick` is processed, then raises it to the power of the full elapsed interval `dt` (up to 7 days). Because utilization can change materially between ticks, the single snapshot rate is applied retroactively to the entire window, over- or under-accruing interest for every borrower and depositor in the pool.

### Finding Description

When the sequencer submits a `SpotTick` transaction, `EndpointTx.processTransactionImpl` computes `dt = txn.time - t.spotTime` and calls `spotEngine.updateStates(dt)`. [1](#0-0) 

Inside `updateStates`, `_updateState` is invoked for every product: [2](#0-1) 

`_updateState` reads `totalBorrows` and `totalDeposits` from the **current** state, derives `utilizationRatioX18`, computes an annualized `borrowerRateX18`, and then applies it for the entire `dt` window via exponentiation: [3](#0-2) 

The resulting `borrowRateMultiplierX18` is then permanently folded into `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18`: [4](#0-3) 

The invariant broken: the utilization ratio is **not** constant over `dt`. Any deposit, withdrawal, borrow, or repayment that occurred between the previous tick and the current tick changes the true time-weighted average rate. Using only the end-of-period snapshot means the entire `dt` is priced at the wrong rate.

### Impact Explanation

`cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` and `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` are the sole source of truth for every subaccount's real balance: [5](#0-4) 

A corrupted multiplier permanently misstates every borrower's debt and every depositor's credit for all future interactions (withdrawals, health checks, liquidations). If utilization spikes just before a tick with a large `dt`, all pre-existing borrowers are retroactively charged at the elevated rate for the full window, transferring wealth from borrowers to depositors. The reverse (utilization drops before a tick) undercharges borrowers and shortchanges depositors.

### Likelihood Explanation

The `SpotTick` interval is sequencer-controlled and bounded only by `require(dt < 7 * SECONDS_PER_DAY)`. [6](#0-5) 

An unprivileged user can observe the on-chain `times.spotTime`, wait until a large gap has accumulated (e.g., 5–7 days of sequencer inactivity or slow-mode delay), then deposit and borrow a large amount to spike utilization immediately before the next tick. The sequencer then submits a `SpotTick` with `dt` equal to the full gap, and `_updateState` applies the attacker-inflated utilization rate retroactively across the entire window. The attacker can simultaneously hold a deposit position to capture the excess interest paid by existing borrowers.

### Recommendation

Replace the single end-of-period snapshot with a time-weighted accumulation. The standard approach is to call `_updateState` (with a small `dt`) on every state-changing operation (deposit, borrow, repay, withdrawal) so that the rate is always computed against the utilization that was actually in effect at that moment. Alternatively, store a running time-weighted utilization integral and use it to derive the average rate over `dt` rather than the instantaneous end-of-period value.

### Proof of Concept

1. At time T0, a `SpotTick` is processed; `times.spotTime = T0`. Utilization is 20%.
2. No `SpotTick` is submitted for 6 days (within the 7-day cap).
3. At time T0 + 6 days − ε, an attacker calls `depositCollateral` then borrows heavily via a matched order, pushing utilization to 90%.
4. The sequencer submits a `SpotTick` with `txn.time = T0 + 6 days`, so `dt = 6 days`.
5. `_updateState` reads `utilizationRatioX18 = 90%`, computes `borrowRateMultiplierX18 = (1 + rate_90%)^(6 days)`, and multiplies `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` by this value.
6. Every borrower who held a position for the entire 6-day window is charged 6 days of interest at the 90% utilization rate, even though the true rate was 20% for nearly the entire period.
7. The attacker repays their borrow (paying only a few seconds of interest at 90%) and withdraws their deposit, having collected the excess interest redistributed to depositors.
