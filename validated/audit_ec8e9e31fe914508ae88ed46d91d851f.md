### Title
Quote Token Price Hardcoded to 1 While Non-Quote Oracle Prices Are USD-Denominated, Causing Health Miscalculation on USDC Depeg — (`core/contracts/SpotEngine.sol`)

---

### Summary

`SpotEngine.initialize()` hardcodes the quote token (`QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID`) price to `ONE` (1e18) and never updates it. All non-quote asset oracle prices (`priceX18`) are denominated in USD and updated by the sequencer. When the quote token (USDC) depegs from $1.00, the health calculation mixes USD-denominated asset values with a stale USDC price of exactly 1, producing incorrect health scores. This is the direct analog of the USSD H-11 bug: oracle prices are in one denomination (USD) while the peg/accounting unit is another (USDC), and the system never reconciles the two.

---

### Finding Description

In `SpotEngine.initialize()`, the quote product risk is set with a hardcoded price:

```solidity
_risk().value[QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID] = RiskHelper.RiskStore({
    longWeightInitial: 1e9,
    shortWeightInitial: 1e9,
    longWeightMaintenance: 1e9,
    shortWeightMaintenance: 1e9,
    priceX18: ONE   // hardcoded, never updated
});
``` [1](#0-0) 

The `updatePrice()` function in `BaseEngine` is only callable by the clearinghouse and is used to update non-quote product prices. The quote product price is never passed through this path. [2](#0-1) 

Health for every subaccount is computed in `BaseEngine._calculateProductHealth()`:

```solidity
health += quoteAmount;
if (amount != 0) {
    health += amount.mul(weight).mul(risk.priceX18);
}
``` [3](#0-2) 

For the quote product itself, `amount` is the USDC balance and `risk.priceX18 = ONE`, so the USDC balance is added at face value (1 USDC = 1 unit). For non-quote products (ETH, BTC), `risk.priceX18` is the USD price updated by the sequencer. The comments in `Constants.sol` confirm the system is denominated in USD (`// $1`, `// $0.1`): [4](#0-3) 

This creates a denomination mismatch: non-quote asset values are in USD, but the quote token is always counted as 1 unit regardless of its real USD value. When USDC ≠ $1.00, the health calculation is wrong.

`getHealth()` in `Clearinghouse.sol` is the single source of truth for all withdrawal, liquidation, and health-check decisions: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**When USDC depegs below $1.00 (e.g., USDC = $0.90, as occurred in March 2023):**

- A user deposits 10,000 USDC (real value: $9,000).
- The system values this collateral at 10,000 units (overvalued by ~11%).
- The user borrows ETH worth 9,000 units at the system's USD oracle price.
- In real terms, the user has borrowed $9,000 of ETH against $9,000 of real collateral — effectively 100% LTV, bypassing the intended margin requirement.
- If the user defaults, the protocol liquidates 10,000 USDC to cover $9,000 of ETH debt. At $0.90/USDC, the 10,000 USDC is only worth $9,000, leaving zero buffer for liquidation fees and price slippage.
- The insurance fund is drained; the protocol becomes insolvent.

**When USDC depegs above $1.00 (e.g., USDC = $1.10):**

- Non-quote collateral (ETH, BTC) is undervalued relative to USDC in the system.
- Users with ETH/BTC collateral are liquidated even though their real collateral value (in USDC) is sufficient.
- Liquidators extract value from solvent users at the protocol's expense.

The `withdrawCollateral` path enforces health via `getHealth()`: [6](#0-5) 

The `liquidateSubaccountImpl` path also relies on the same health check: [7](#0-6) 

Both are corrupted by the stale USDC price.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

USDC has depegged materially in the past (March 2023 SVB crisis: USDC traded at $0.87 on secondary markets). Any user can trigger this by simply depositing USDC and borrowing non-quote assets — no special permissions, no admin access, no sequencer compromise required. The entry path is the standard `depositCollateral` → `withdrawCollateral` flow available to all users.

---

### Recommendation

Oracle prices for non-quote assets should be denominated in USDC (the actual quote token), not USD. Alternatively, the sequencer should update the quote token's `priceX18` using a USDC/USD oracle feed, so that when USDC depegs, the health calculation correctly reflects the real value of USDC collateral. The hardcoded `priceX18: ONE` in `SpotEngine.initialize()` should be replaced with a live oracle-fed price, consistent with how all other product prices are maintained. [1](#0-0) 

---

### Proof of Concept

1. USDC depegs to $0.90 (real-world precedent: March 2023).
2. Attacker acquires 10,000 USDC for $9,000 on an external market.
3. Attacker calls `depositCollateral(QUOTE_PRODUCT_ID, 10_000e6)` via `Endpoint`.
4. `SpotEngine` records 10,000e18 units of USDC balance. `getHealth()` returns 10,000e18 (USDC price = ONE).
5. Attacker calls to borrow ETH worth 9,000 units (90% of collateral, within initial margin).
6. `getHealth()` passes: 10,000 − 9,000 × shortWeightInitial ≥ 0.
7. Attacker withdraws ETH and sells on external market for $9,000 USD = 10,000 USDC.
8. Attacker walks away from the Nado position.
9. Protocol attempts liquidation: 10,000 USDC collateral covers 9,000 units of ETH debt.
10. At $0.90/USDC, 10,000 USDC = $9,000 real value = exactly the ETH debt value, leaving zero margin for liquidation fees (`LIQUIDATION_FEE_FRACTION = 50%` of spread) or price impact.
11. Insurance fund is consumed; protocol is insolvent.
