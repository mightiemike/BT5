### Title
Global `lastLiquidationFees` Cross-Subaccount Contamination Triggers Premature Spot Socialization — (`core/contracts/ClearinghouseLiq.sol`)

---

### Summary

`lastLiquidationFees` is a **global** state variable in `ClearinghouseStorage`. It is written by every non-finalize liquidation step, regardless of which subaccount is being liquidated. When `_finalizeSubaccount` is called for subaccount B, it subtracts `lastLiquidationFees` from `insurance` to decide whether to call `spotEngine.socializeSubaccount`. If the last non-finalize liquidation was for a **different** subaccount A and generated large fees, the subtraction makes `v.insurance` appear insufficient, triggering socialization of B's deficit even though the protocol's insurance fund had more than enough to cover it.

---

### Finding Description

`lastLiquidationFees` is declared as a plain `int128` in `ClearinghouseStorage`: [1](#0-0) 

It is written unconditionally at the end of every non-finalize liquidation in `_handleLiquidationPayment`: [2](#0-1) 

The comment at lines 581–585 explains the design intent: the last liquidation step of a subaccount adds fees to `insurance`, which would otherwise prevent socialization from triggering. The subtraction is meant to "undo" that last fee addition for the **same** subaccount. But the implementation is global — it undoes the last fee addition from **any** subaccount.

In `_finalizeSubaccount`, the socialization gate is: [3](#0-2) 

The sequence is:
1. `v.insurance = insurance - lastLiquidationFees` (line 369)
2. Use `v.insurance` to cover perp losses and quote deficit
3. `if (v.insurance <= 0) { spotEngine.socializeSubaccount(txn.liquidatee); }` (line 407–409)
4. `v.insurance += lastLiquidationFees` (line 410) — adds back the fees
5. `insurance = v.insurance` (line 411)

`spotEngine.socializeSubaccount` reduces `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` for every spot product where the liquidatee has a negative balance: [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

When `lastLiquidationFees` is large (from a prior liquidation of a different subaccount), `v.insurance` is artificially deflated. The `insuranceCover` applied to the liquidatee's quote deficit is capped at this deflated value, leaving a residual negative balance that triggers `spotEngine.socializeSubaccount`. This reduces `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` across all depositors — a permanent, protocol-wide loss of depositor funds — even though the insurance fund had sufficient balance to cover the entire deficit without socialization.

After socialization, `v.insurance += lastLiquidationFees` restores the full insurance balance (line 410), confirming that the insurance was never actually exhausted. The socialization was entirely spurious.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attacker must be a liquidator with two liquidatable subaccounts available: one with a large position (to generate large `lastLiquidationFees`) and one with a deficit in the range `(I_before, I_before + F]`. No sequencer compromise is required — the attacker submits two sequential `LiquidateSubaccount` transactions through the normal endpoint flow. The attacker can engineer the large-fee liquidation by holding or acquiring a large position that becomes liquidatable, or by waiting for natural market conditions. The attack is locally reproducible on an unmodified Hardhat setup.

---

### Recommendation

Scope `lastLiquidationFees` to the subaccount being finalized. Replace the global `int128 lastLiquidationFees` with a `mapping(bytes32 => int128) lastLiquidationFees` keyed by `txn.liquidatee`, so that the fee subtraction in `_finalizeSubaccount` only undoes the last fee addition for that specific subaccount. Alternatively, reset `lastLiquidationFees` to zero at the start of each `liquidateSubaccountImpl` call and only set it within the same call's `_handleLiquidationPayment` invocation.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:**
- `insurance = 5e18` (5 USDC)
- `lastLiquidationFees = 0`

**Step 1 — Attacker liquidates subaccount A (large perp position):**
- `v.liquidationFees = 100e18` (large, attacker chose a large `txn.amount`)
- After `_handleLiquidationPayment`: `insurance = 105e18`, `lastLiquidationFees = 100e18`

**Step 2 — Attacker calls `liquidateSubaccountImpl(liquidatee=B, productId=uint32.max)`:**
- B has all perp/spot positions closed, quote balance = `-10e18`
- `_finalizeSubaccount` executes:
  - `v.insurance = 105e18 - 100e18 = 5e18`
  - `perpEngine.socializeSubaccount(B, 5e18)` → returns `5e18` (no perp losses)
  - `insuranceCover = min(5e18, 10e18) = 5e18`
  - `v.insurance = 5e18 - 5e18 = 0`
  - `v.insurance <= 0` → **`spotEngine.socializeSubaccount(B)` called**
  - B's remaining `-5e18` quote deficit is socialized: `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` decreases for USDC
  - `v.insurance += 100e18 = 100e18`
  - `insurance = 100e18`

**Invariant broken:** After the call, `insurance = 100e18` — the fund was never exhausted. The 5e18 socialized loss was borne by all depositors unnecessarily. The correct outcome (without the cross-subaccount contamination) would have been `insuranceCover = min(105e18, 10e18) = 10e18`, fully covering B's deficit with no socialization.

**Assertion:** Before and after the finalization, `sum(denormalized balances) = totalDepositsNormalized * cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` holds, but `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` has been reduced — meaning every depositor's withdrawable balance is permanently smaller, despite the insurance fund remaining solvent.
