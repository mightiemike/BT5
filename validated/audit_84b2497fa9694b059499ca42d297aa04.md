### Title
Deployer-Controlled `delistProduct()` Bypasses Multisig Governance for Critical Perp Settlement — (File: `core/contracts/ContractOwner.sol`)

---

### Summary

`ContractOwner.delistProduct()` is gated by `onlyDeployer` — a single EOA set at initialization — rather than `onlyOwner` (the multisig). This allows the deployer to unilaterally queue a `DelistProduct` slow-mode transaction that force-settles perp positions for an arbitrary set of subaccounts, bypassing the multisig governance that controls all other critical protocol operations.

---

### Finding Description

`ContractOwner` stores two distinct privileged roles:

- `owner` — set to the multisig via `transferOwnership(multisig)` at initialization
- `deployer` — set to `msg.sender` (the deploying EOA) at initialization and never rotatable [1](#0-0) 

The `onlyDeployer` modifier gates the following functions:

- `submitSpotAddOrUpdateProductCall()` (line 91)
- `submitPerpAddOrUpdateProductCall()` (line 117)
- `clearSpotAddOrUpdateProductCalls()` (line 139)
- `clearPerpAddOrUpdateProductCalls()` (line 143)
- **`delistProduct()`** (line 361) [2](#0-1) 

The most impactful of these is `delistProduct()`. The deployer calls it with a list of `productIds`, `pricesX18`, and `subaccounts`, and it submits a `DelistProduct` slow-mode transaction to the `Endpoint` for each product: [3](#0-2) 

When the sequencer processes this slow-mode transaction, `Clearinghouse.delistProduct()` is invoked, which zeroes out the perp `amount` balance for every listed subaccount and credits/debits their `vQuoteBalance` at the provided settlement price: [4](#0-3) 

The only on-chain constraint is that `priceX18` must equal the current oracle price at the time of processing: [5](#0-4) 

The deployer controls the timing of submission and the set of subaccounts to include, and the slow-mode transaction is queued with a 3-day delay before execution. No multisig approval is required at any step.

The `Endpoint.submitSlowModeTransactionImpl()` does check `require(sender == owner())` for `DelistProduct` transactions, but `sender` here is `msg.sender` of the `submitSlowModeTransaction` call — which is the `ContractOwner` contract itself (not the deployer EOA directly). Since `ContractOwner` is the registered owner of the `Endpoint`, this check passes whenever `ContractOwner.delistProduct()` is called, regardless of whether the caller is the deployer or the multisig: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Severity: Low**

The deployer EOA can unilaterally force-settle perp positions for any set of subaccounts at the current oracle price. While the price constraint prevents arbitrary price manipulation, the deployer can:

1. Choose the timing of delisting to coincide with an unfavorable oracle price for targeted subaccounts.
2. Selectively include or exclude subaccounts from the settlement list, creating asymmetric outcomes.
3. Trigger product delisting without any multisig approval or governance vote.

This is a critical protocol operation — equivalent in impact to `setVoterAndDistributor()` in the Infrared analog — that permanently alters user positions and should require the same security posture as the multisig.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Likelihood: High**

The `deployer` address is permanently set at initialization with no rotation mechanism. Every call to `delistProduct()` always goes through the deployer key. If the deployer key is compromised or acts maliciously, there is no on-chain mechanism to prevent a `DelistProduct` transaction from being queued. The 3-day slow-mode delay provides a detection window but not a prevention mechanism, since there is no cancellation path available to the multisig once the transaction is queued.

---

### Recommendation

Replace `onlyDeployer` with `onlyOwner` on `delistProduct()` in `ContractOwner.sol`, consistent with how all other high-impact protocol operations (`withdrawInsurance`, `addEngine`, `assignPubKey`, `spotUpdateRisk`, etc.) are gated. If a separate operational role is needed, introduce a rotatable role controlled by the multisig rather than a fixed deployer address. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Proof of Concept

1. At deployment, `ContractOwner.initialize()` is called with `msg.sender` as `_deployer`. The deployer EOA is stored permanently.
2. The multisig becomes `owner`; the deployer EOA retains `onlyDeployer` access.
3. The deployer calls `ContractOwner.delistProduct([perpProductId], [currentOraclePrice], [targetSubaccounts])`.
4. `_submitSlowModeTransaction(DelistProduct, ...)` calls `endpoint.submitSlowModeTransaction(...)` with `ContractOwner` as `msg.sender`.
5. `Endpoint.submitSlowModeTransactionImpl` checks `require(sender == owner())` — passes because `ContractOwner` is the Endpoint's owner.
6. The `DelistProduct` slow-mode transaction is queued with a 3-day delay.
7. After the delay, the sequencer processes it: `Clearinghouse.delistProduct()` zeroes out all perp balances for the listed subaccounts and settles them at the oracle price — without any multisig approval having been required. [8](#0-7) [9](#0-8)
