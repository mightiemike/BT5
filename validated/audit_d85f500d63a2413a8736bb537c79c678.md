### Title
No Supply Cap on Spot Assets Enables Unliquidatable Large Positions Leading to Bad Debt — (File: `core/contracts/Clearinghouse.sol`)

---

### Summary

`depositCollateral` in `Clearinghouse.sol` and the `SpotEngine.Config` struct impose no upper bound on total spot asset deposits. A borrow-utilization check exists (`assertUtilization`) but it only enforces `totalDeposits ≥ totalBorrows`; it does not cap absolute supply. This mirrors the external report's root cause exactly: a borrow limit is present, a supply limit is absent. When a large position in a low-liquidity spot asset is built up and then falls into liquidation, the liquidator receives a large block of that asset at oracle-discounted price but cannot sell it profitably on external markets due to price impact. This disincentivizes liquidator participation, leaves the position underwater, and ultimately forces bad-debt socialization onto depositors.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause 1 — No supply cap at deposit time.**

`depositCollateral` in `Clearinghouse.sol` performs only a decimal-normalization and a balance update; there is no check against any per-product maximum supply: [1](#0-0) 

**Root cause 2 — `SpotEngine.Config` carries no `maxSupply` field.**

The full config struct for every spot product is: [2](#0-1) 

Fields present: `token`, `interestInflectionUtilX18`, `interestFloorX18`, `interestSmallCapX18`, `interestLargeCapX18`, `withdrawFeeX18`, `minDepositRateX18`. No supply ceiling exists.

**Root cause 3 — `assertUtilization` is a borrow cap, not a supply cap.**

The only protocol-level guard on pool size is: [3](#0-2) 

This is called only during `withdrawCollateral` and only enforces `totalDeposits ≥ totalBorrows`. It does not prevent `totalDeposits` from growing without bound.

**Liquidation settlement path.**

When a position is liquidated, `_handleLiquidationPayment` transfers the full `txn.amount` of spot asset to the liquidator at `liquidationPriceX18` (oracle price minus a discount): [4](#0-3) 

The protocol assumes the liquidator can absorb and re-sell this block at or near oracle price. If the block is large relative to external market depth, the liquidator's realized exit price will be materially below oracle price, making the liquidation unprofitable.

**Bad-debt path when liquidators abstain.**

If no liquidator participates, the position remains under maintenance health. Eventually `_finalizeSubaccount` is invoked, which calls `socializeSubaccount` on the spot engine: [5](#0-4) 

`socializeSubaccount` in `SpotEngine.sol` reduces `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` for every depositor in the affected product, permanently diluting their balances: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

- **Depositor loss**: `socializeSubaccount` reduces `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`, meaning every depositor in the product loses a proportional share of their balance. This is a direct, irreversible asset loss.
- **Insurance fund depletion**: Before socialization, the insurance fund is consumed to cover the shortfall (`insuranceCover` path in `_finalizeSubaccount`), reducing the protocol's backstop for future events.
- **Cascading risk**: A single large position in a low-liquidity asset can exhaust the insurance fund and trigger socialization across all depositors of that product.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The Nado protocol is designed to list multiple spot assets (`addOrUpdateProduct` is permissioned but routine). Any non-major asset with limited on-chain liquidity is a candidate. A user (or coordinated set of users) can deposit an unbounded amount of such an asset, borrow against it, and allow the position to become undercollateralized during a price decline. The liquidation block size is bounded only by the liquidatee's position, not by any protocol-enforced supply ceiling. This is a realistic scenario for any long-tail spot asset listed on the protocol.

---

### Recommendation

1. Add a `maxSupply` (or `supplyCapX18`) field to `ISpotEngine.Config`.
2. In `depositCollateral` (and in `updateBalance` when called from non-liquidation paths), assert that `totalDepositsNormalized × cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18 ≤ maxSupply` after the update.
3. Set the cap conservatively relative to the on-chain and off-chain liquidity depth of each listed asset, and update it as market conditions change.
4. The existing `assertUtilization` borrow check should be retained as a complementary guard.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Owner lists a low-liquidity spot asset `TOKEN_X` via `SpotEngine.addOrUpdateProduct` with no supply ceiling.
2. Attacker deposits `10_000_000e18` units of `TOKEN_X` via `Endpoint → Clearinghouse.depositCollateral`. No supply check fires.
3. Attacker borrows USDC against `TOKEN_X` up to the initial health limit.
4. `TOKEN_X` price drops 30%; attacker's maintenance health goes negative.
5. A liquidator evaluates the liquidation: to liquidate the full position they must receive `10_000_000e18` units of `TOKEN_X`. Selling this block on any available venue would move the market by 40–60%, making the net proceeds less than the quote paid. The liquidation is unprofitable; the liquidator abstains.
6. No liquidator participates. The sequencer eventually submits `liquidateSubaccountImpl` with `productId = type(uint32).max` (finalize path).
7. `_finalizeSubaccount` consumes the insurance fund and calls `spotEngine.socializeSubaccount(liquidatee)`.
8. `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` for `TOKEN_X` is reduced, permanently diluting all `TOKEN_X` depositors' balances.
