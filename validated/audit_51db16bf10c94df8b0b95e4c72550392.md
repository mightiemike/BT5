### Title
Stale Interest Multiplier on Slow-Mode Deposit Dilutes Existing Depositors' Yield — (File: `core/contracts/SpotEngineState.sol`, `core/contracts/Endpoint.sol`)

---

### Summary

`SpotEngine.updateStates()` — which advances the `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` to accrue interest — is only ever called by the sequencer via a `SpotTick` transaction. When a user executes a slow-mode deposit through `Endpoint.executeSlowModeTransaction()`, no `updateStates` call precedes the balance update. If the sequencer has been inactive (the exact scenario slow mode is designed for), the multiplier is stale. The new depositor receives a larger normalized share than they are entitled to, and when the sequencer eventually resumes and calls `SpotTick`, the new depositor retroactively captures interest that accrued before they joined — stealing yield from existing depositors.

---

### Finding Description

**Interest accrual mechanism.** `SpotEngine` tracks depositor balances in normalized form:

```
amountNormalized = actualDeposit / cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18
```

The multiplier grows over time as interest accrues. A depositor's real balance at any moment is:

```
actualBalance = amountNormalized × cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18
```

`_updateState()` advances the multiplier, and it is only invoked from `updateStates()`: [1](#0-0) 

`updateStates()` itself is only reachable via a `SpotTick` transaction submitted by the sequencer: [2](#0-1) 

**Deposit path does not call `updateStates`.** When a slow-mode deposit is executed, the call chain terminates in `SpotEngine.updateBalance()`, which calls `_updateBalanceNormalized()` directly — with no preceding call to `updateStates()`: [3](#0-2) 

**Slow-mode execution is user-controlled.** After the hardcoded three-day delay, any caller can invoke `executeSlowModeTransaction()`: [4](#0-3) 

The sequencer is not required to be online for this call to succeed. This is the censorship-resistance guarantee of slow mode.

**The stale-multiplier dilution.** Suppose the multiplier has not been updated for `T` seconds (sequencer down). At the moment of the slow-mode deposit execution:

- The stored multiplier `M_stale` is lower than the true current value `M_true`.
- The new depositor receives `amountNormalized = deposit / M_stale`, which is **larger** than the correct `deposit / M_true`.
- When the sequencer resumes and calls `SpotTick` with the accumulated `dt`, the multiplier jumps from `M_stale` to `M_true`.
- The new depositor's balance becomes `(deposit / M_stale) × M_true > deposit`, capturing interest that accrued entirely before they joined.
- Existing depositors' share of that same interest is proportionally reduced.

This is structurally identical to the Reserve Protocol bug: a state-update that should precede a new deposit (`_payoutRewards()` / `updateStates()`) is skipped, allowing the new entrant to immediately claim a share of accumulated but undistributed yield.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Asset delta:** Existing depositors lose a fraction of their accrued interest proportional to the new depositor's share of the pool. The stolen amount scales with:
- The total deposits in the pool.
- The interest rate configured for the product.
- The duration `T` since the last `SpotTick` (i.e., how long the sequencer was inactive).

For a pool with significant TVL and a multi-day sequencer outage, the stolen yield can be material. The corrupted invariant is: `amountNormalized` for a new depositor should reflect the multiplier at the time of deposit, not a stale pre-accrual multiplier.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Slow mode is explicitly designed for sequencer downtime or censorship. An attacker who anticipates or causes a sequencer outage can:
1. Submit a slow-mode deposit just before or during the outage.
2. Execute it after the three-day delay, while the sequencer is still down.
3. Wait for the sequencer to resume and call `SpotTick`, at which point the stolen yield is crystallized.

The three-day delay is a cost, but it is not a deterrent for a patient attacker targeting a high-TVL pool. Likelihood is **Low-Medium** — it requires sequencer inactivity, but that is precisely the condition slow mode is designed to handle.

---

### Recommendation

Before crediting a new deposit to a subaccount's normalized balance, the interest state for the relevant product should be brought up to date. Concretely, `SpotEngine` should expose an internal path to apply accrued interest (using the current oracle time as the reference) before `_updateBalanceNormalized` is called during slow-mode deposit processing. This mirrors the Reserve Protocol's recommended fix of calling `poke()` before pausing, ensuring the multiplier is current before any new entrant's share is computed.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Depositor A deposits 1,000 USDC at `t = 0`. Sequencer calls `SpotTick` normally; multiplier `M = 1.0`.
2. At `t = 1 day`, the sequencer goes offline. No further `SpotTick` is submitted. The multiplier remains `M = 1.0` on-chain, even though the true accrued value would be `M ≈ 1.001` (assuming ~0.1%/day rate).
3. Attacker B calls `depositCollateral()` at `t = 1 day`, submitting a slow-mode deposit of 1,000 USDC. The slow-mode tx is queued with `executableAt = t + 3 days = t = 4 days`.
4. At `t = 4 days`, B calls `executeSlowModeTransaction()`. The sequencer is still offline; `M` is still `1.0` on-chain. B's normalized balance is set to `1000 / 1.0 = 1000`.
5. At `t = 5 days`, the sequencer resumes and submits `SpotTick` with `dt = 5 days`. The multiplier jumps to `M ≈ 1.005`.
   - A's balance: `1000 × 1.005 = 1005` (gained 5 USDC).
   - B's balance: `1000 × 1.005 = 1005` (gained 5 USDC).
   - **Correct outcome** (had `updateStates` been called before B's deposit): A should have gained ~5 USDC; B, who was only a depositor for 1 day, should have gained ~1 USDC.
6. B has stolen ~4 USDC of interest from A without having been a depositor during the accrual period.

The root cause is that `updateBalance` in `SpotEngine` does not call `updateStates` before computing the new depositor's normalized share: [3](#0-2) 

and `updateStates` is exclusively gated behind the sequencer-only `SpotTick` path: [2](#0-1)
