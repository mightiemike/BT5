### Title
Hardcoded Magic Number `31536000` as Seconds-Per-Year Divisor Causes Systematic Interest Rate Miscalculation — (`File: core/contracts/SpotEngineState.sol`)

---

### Summary

`SpotEngineState._updateState` converts annualized borrow and minimum-deposit rates to per-second rates by dividing by the inline magic number `31536000` (365 × 86400). This value is never defined as a named constant, is not derived from the existing `SECONDS_PER_DAY` constant in `Constants.sol`, and is not documented anywhere in the codebase. The choice of 365 days vs. 365.25 days (or any other convention) is undocumented and unverifiable, making this a direct analog to the `EPOCH_SECONDS` bug: a hardcoded time-duration constant whose exact value is assumed rather than specified, and which silently corrupts every interest accrual calculation in the protocol.

---

### Finding Description

In `SpotEngineState._updateState`, the annualized `borrowerRateX18` is converted to a per-second rate by dividing by `MathSD21x18.fromInt(31536000)`: [1](#0-0) 

The same literal appears again when converting `minDepositRateX18` to a per-second rate: [2](#0-1) 

`31536000` equals exactly 365 × 86400. However:

1. The codebase already defines `SECONDS_PER_DAY = 3600 * 24` in `Constants.sol` — `31536000` is not derived from it and is not defined as a named constant anywhere.
2. No comment, documentation, or named constant explains whether the protocol intends a 365-day year, a 365.25-day year (accounting for leap years), or any other convention.
3. The value appears as a bare integer literal in two separate locations, with no single source of truth. [3](#0-2) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Every call to `_updateState` — triggered by the sequencer via `updateStates` on the `SpotEngine` — uses this divisor to compute the per-second borrow rate multiplier and the per-second minimum deposit rate multiplier. Both multipliers are then applied to `cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` and `cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18`, which are the on-chain accumulators that determine every depositor's earned interest and every borrower's owed interest across all spot products.

If the intended convention is 365.25 days/year (31,557,600 seconds), the current divisor is ~0.068% too small, meaning the per-second rate is ~0.068% too high. Compounded over a year across all spot products, every borrower overpays and every depositor over-earns relative to the documented annualized rate. For a protocol with large total deposits and borrows, this is a continuous, silent accounting drift that accumulates without bound and cannot be corrected without a migration.

If the convention is correct for non-leap years but wrong for leap years (31,622,400 seconds), the error is ~0.274% of the annualized rate for that year.

The corrupted state variables are `state.cumulativeBorrowsMultiplierX18` and `state.cumulativeDepositsMultiplierX18` for every spot product. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

This is triggered on every sequencer call to `updateStates` on the `SpotEngine`, which is a normal, continuous protocol operation. No special attacker action is required — the miscalculation is structural and fires unconditionally whenever utilization is nonzero or `minDepositRateX18` is nonzero. Likelihood is **high** (it is already happening on every state update).

---

### Recommendation

1. Define a named constant `SECONDS_PER_YEAR` in `core/contracts/common/Constants.sol`, derived from `SECONDS_PER_DAY`, and document the chosen convention (e.g., `uint64 constant SECONDS_PER_YEAR = 365 * SECONDS_PER_DAY;`).
2. Replace both inline occurrences of `MathSD21x18.fromInt(31536000)` in `SpotEngineState._updateState` with `MathSD21x18.fromInt(int256(SECONDS_PER_YEAR))`.
3. Confirm in documentation whether the protocol intends a 365-day or 365.25-day year and set the constant accordingly.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Concrete miscalculation example:**

Assume `interestFloorX18 = 1e16` (1% annualized floor rate) and `utilizationRatioX18 = 0` (so `borrowerRateX18 = 0` per the zero-utilization branch — but for `minDepositRateX18 != 0`, the path at line 149 fires unconditionally).

Suppose `minDepositRateX18 = 5e16` (5% annualized minimum deposit rate):

- Current per-second rate: `5e16 / 31536000 ≈ 1585489.6 wei` (in X18 fixed-point)
- Correct per-second rate (365.25 days): `5e16 / 31557600 ≈ 1584423.4 wei`
- Difference per second: ~1066 wei in X18 fixed-point ≈ **0.068% excess rate**

Over one year with `totalDeposits = 10,000,000e18` ($10M), the excess interest accrued to depositors (and charged to borrowers) is approximately **$6,800** — a direct, continuous accounting error that grows with protocol TVL and cannot be self-correcting. [5](#0-4)
