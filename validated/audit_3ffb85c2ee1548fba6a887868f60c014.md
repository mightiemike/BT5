### Title
`submit_blocks` Always Reverts During Pause for `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` Callers Due to Incompatible Inner `run_mainchain_gc` Pause Guard — (File: `contract/src/lib.rs`)

### Summary

`submit_blocks` is explicitly designed to remain callable when the contract is paused, via the `bypass_roles(Role::DAO, Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks)` exception on the `#[trusted_relayer]` macro. However, `submit_blocks` unconditionally calls `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)` internally, and `run_mainchain_gc` carries its own independent pause guard `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]` that only exempts `UnrestrictedRunGC` holders. Because `env::predecessor_account_id()` is the original external caller throughout the entire NEAR transaction, the inner pause check fires against the caller's actual roles. A caller holding `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` (but not `UnrestrictedRunGC`) will always be rejected by the inner guard, causing `submit_blocks` to revert unconditionally during any pause — making the `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role completely non-functional.

### Finding Description

`submit_blocks` carries two access-control decorators:

- `#[pause]` — blocks paused callers
- `#[trusted_relayer]` — with `bypass_roles(Role::DAO, Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks)`, which is the mechanism that allows privileged relayers to submit blocks even while the contract is paused [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

Inside `submit_blocks`, after processing all headers, the function unconditionally calls `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)`: [3](#0-2) 

`run_mainchain_gc` is a public method decorated with its own independent pause guard: [4](#0-3) 

The `near-plugins` `#[pause]` macro injects a role check at the top of the function body that reads `env::predecessor_account_id()` — the original external caller — and verifies it holds `UnrestrictedRunGC`. This check fires identically whether the function is called externally or internally via `self.run_mainchain_gc(...)`, because in NEAR there is no separate "internal call" context: `predecessor_account_id` is the original transaction signer throughout.

The two role sets are disjoint by design:

| Role | Bypasses `submit_blocks` pause | Bypasses `run_mainchain_gc` pause |
|---|---|---|
| `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` | Yes (via `bypass_roles`) | **No** |
| `UnrestrictedRunGC` | No | Yes |
| `DAO` | Yes (via `bypass_roles`) | **No** | [5](#0-4) 

A caller with `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` (the intended relayer role) passes the outer `submit_blocks` guard but is then rejected by the inner `run_mainchain_gc` guard, causing the entire transaction to revert.

### Impact Explanation

The `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role exists precisely to keep the relayer operational during a pause (e.g., during an emergency or upgrade). With this bug, that role is completely inert: every `submit_blocks` call by such a relayer reverts during a pause. The light client's `mainchain_tip_blockhash` and `mainchain_height_to_header` index stop advancing. Any downstream consumer calling `verify_transaction_inclusion` or `verify_transaction_inclusion_v2` for blocks mined after the pause began will receive a panic ("block does not belong to the current main chain" or "Not enough blocks confirmed"), because the on-chain tip is stale. The SPV proof API is effectively broken for recent transactions for the entire duration of the pause. [6](#0-5) 

### Likelihood Explanation

The pause mechanism is a standard operational tool used during upgrades or incident response. The `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role is explicitly documented and granted to relayer accounts. Any operator who pauses the contract and expects the relayer to continue operating will trigger this failure on the very first `submit_blocks` call. The trigger requires no adversarial input — it fires for any valid batch of headers submitted by a legitimately credentialed relayer while the contract is paused.

### Recommendation

Remove the `#[pause]` guard from `run_mainchain_gc` when it is called internally from `submit_blocks`, or extract the GC logic into a private helper function (e.g., `run_mainchain_gc_inner`) that has no pause guard, and call that helper from `submit_blocks`. The public `run_mainchain_gc` entry point can retain its own `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]` guard for direct external callers. Alternatively, add `Role::UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` and `Role::DAO` to the `except` list of `run_mainchain_gc`'s pause guard so that callers who can bypass `submit_blocks`'s pause can also bypass the inner guard.

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy the contract and grant account `relayer.near` the `UnrestrictedSubmitBlocks` role (but not `UnrestrictedRunGC`).
2. Submit enough blocks so that `mainchain_size > gc_threshold` (so GC will actually attempt to remove blocks when triggered).
3. A `PauseManager` calls `pa_pause_feature` to pause the contract.
4. `relayer.near` calls `submit_blocks` with a valid next block header and sufficient deposit.
5. The `#[trusted_relayer]` bypass allows the call past the outer `#[pause]` guard on `submit_blocks`.
6. `submit_blocks` processes the header successfully, then reaches line 181: `self.run_mainchain_gc(num_of_headers)`.
7. `run_mainchain_gc`'s `#[pause(except(roles(Role::UnrestrictedRunGC)))]` guard checks `env::predecessor_account_id()` = `relayer.near`, finds it lacks `UnrestrictedRunGC`, and panics with the pause error.
8. The entire transaction reverts. The chain tip is not updated. The relayer cannot make progress for the duration of the pause. [7](#0-6) [4](#0-3)
