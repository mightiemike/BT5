### Title
`burnNlp` Uses Maintenance Health Check Instead of Initial, Eliminating Safety Buffer and Enabling Forced Liquidation — (`core/contracts/Clearinghouse.sol`)

---

### Summary

The `burnNlp` function in `Clearinghouse.sol` enforces only a `MAINTENANCE` health check after reducing a user's collateral. Every other user-initiated collateral-reducing action (`withdrawCollateral`, `transferQuote`) enforces the stricter `INITIAL` health check. Because `MAINTENANCE` is the same threshold used to trigger liquidation, a user who burns NLP to the maximum permitted amount is placed exactly at the liquidation boundary with zero safety buffer. Any subsequent adverse price movement — including one engineered by an attacker through normal trading — immediately makes the account liquidatable.

---

### Finding Description

Nado's health system has two distinct thresholds:

- **`INITIAL`** — the stricter threshold, enforced when a user opens a position or withdraws collateral. `longWeightInitialX18 < longWeightMaintenanceX18` for long positions, so INITIAL health is always lower than MAINTENANCE health for the same portfolio. This gap is the intended safety buffer.
- **`MAINTENANCE`** — the looser threshold, used exclusively to determine whether an account is liquidatable.

`withdrawCollateral` correctly uses `INITIAL`:

```solidity
IProductEngine.HealthType healthType = sender == X_ACCOUNT
    ? IProductEngine.HealthType.PNL
    : IProductEngine.HealthType.INITIAL;
require(getHealth(sender, healthType) >= 0, ERR_SUBACCT_HEALTH);
```

`transferQuote` also correctly uses `INITIAL`:

```solidity
require(_isAboveInitial(txn.sender), ERR_SUBACCT_HEALTH);
```

`burnNlp`, however, uses `MAINTENANCE`:

```solidity
require(
    getHealth(txn.sender, IProductEngine.HealthType.MAINTENANCE) >= 0,
    ERR_SUBACCT_HEALTH
);
```

This means a user can burn NLP tokens until their MAINTENANCE health reaches exactly 0. At that point, their INITIAL health is already negative (they are already "under initial"), and their MAINTENANCE health is at the liquidation boundary. Any small price movement — or a deliberate trade by an attacker on the `OffchainExchange` — pushes MAINTENANCE below 0, making the account immediately liquidatable via `liquidateSubaccountImpl`.

The code comment acknowledges the risk ("Burning NLP can decrease health if the burn fee exceeds the health improvement from the withdrawal") but the chosen mitigation (MAINTENANCE check) eliminates the buffer rather than preserving it.

---

### Impact Explanation

A user who burns NLP to the maximum amount permitted by the MAINTENANCE check is placed at the liquidation boundary with no buffer. An attacker can:

1. Observe (or induce) a victim burning NLP to near-maximum.
2. Execute a trade on `OffchainExchange` that moves the oracle price slightly against the victim's open positions.
3. Submit a `LiquidateSubaccount` transaction, collecting the liquidation discount as profit.

The liquidator receives `liquidationFees` credited to `insurance` and a discounted asset price, making this profitable. The victim suffers an unfair liquidation despite having acted within what the protocol allowed.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any user who holds NLP and has open positions can be affected. The `BurnNlp` transaction type is a standard user-initiated action processed through `EndpointTx.processTransactionImpl`. No privileged access is required. The attacker only needs to observe the victim's on-chain state (subaccount balances are readable) and execute a normal trade to shift the price. This is a realistic, low-cost attack on any user who burns NLP aggressively.

---

### Recommendation

Replace the `MAINTENANCE` health check in `burnNlp` with an `INITIAL` health check, consistent with every other user-initiated collateral-reducing action:

```solidity
require(
    getHealth(txn.sender, IProductEngine.HealthType.INITIAL) >= 0,
    ERR_SUBACCT_HEALTH
);
```

This restores the intended safety buffer (`longWeightMaintenance > longWeightInitial`) between the maximum burn point and the liquidation threshold.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice holds an open perp long position and NLP tokens. Her portfolio: INITIAL health = +500, MAINTENANCE health = +800.
2. Alice submits a `BurnNlp` transaction. The MAINTENANCE check allows her to burn until MAINTENANCE = 0. After burning, her state is: INITIAL health = −300, MAINTENANCE health = 0.
3. Eve observes Alice's subaccount on-chain. Eve submits a sell order on `OffchainExchange` for the same perp product, moving the oracle price down by 0.1%.
4. Alice's MAINTENANCE health is now −ε < 0. `isUnderMaintenance(alice)` returns `true`.
5. Eve submits a `LiquidateSubaccount` transaction. `liquidateSubaccountImpl` passes all checks, transfers Alice's position to Eve at a discount, and credits `liquidationFees` to the insurance fund (and Eve's net position).
6. Eve swaps back the acquired asset, profiting from the liquidation discount.
