### Title
Liquidations Exclusively Gated Behind Sequencer With No Permissionless Fallback — (`File: core/contracts/Endpoint.sol`, `File: core/contracts/EndpointTx.sol`)

---

### Summary
Nado's `LiquidateSubaccount` transaction type can only be executed through the sequencer-gated fast path. There is no slow-mode or permissionless on-chain path for any external actor to trigger a liquidation if the sequencer is unavailable. This mirrors the external report's single-bot design: the sequencer is the sole actor capable of processing liquidations, and its failure leaves underwater positions unliquidated.

---

### Finding Description

All fast-mode transactions, including `LiquidateSubaccount`, are submitted via `submitTransactionsChecked` in `Endpoint.sol`. This function enforces a hard sequencer-only gate:

```solidity
// Endpoint.sol:279
require(msg.sender == sequencer);
```

The slow-mode path (`submitSlowModeTransaction` → `submitSlowModeTransactionImpl` → `processSlowModeTransactionImpl`) does not handle `LiquidateSubaccount`. In `processSlowModeTransactionImpl`, the handled transaction types are `DepositCollateral`, `WithdrawCollateral`, `DepositInsurance`, `LinkSigner`, `DumpFees`, `RebalanceXWithdraw`, `UpdateTierFeeRates`, `AddNlpPool`, `UpdateNlpPool`, `DeleteNlpPool`, `ForceRebalanceNlpPool`, `NlpProfitShare`, `UpdateBuilder`, and `ClaimBuilderFee`. `LiquidateSubaccount` is absent from this list and hits the terminal `revert()` at the end of the function.

A user who attempts to queue a `LiquidateSubaccount` via slow mode would pay the `SLOW_MODE_FEE` ($1 USDC) and have the transaction silently discarded when processed (the `try/catch` in `_executeSlowModeTransaction` swallows the revert on non-hardhat chains).

The `processTransactionImpl` in `EndpointTx.sol` does handle `LiquidateSubaccount` (including signature validation for non-`N_ACCOUNT` senders), but this function is only reachable via `delegatecall` from `Endpoint.processTransaction`, which is itself only called from `submitTransactionsChecked` (sequencer-only) or `submitTransactionsCheckedWithGasLimit` (which always terminates with `verifier.revertGasInfo` — a gas-estimation-only path that never commits state).

---

### Impact Explanation

If the sequencer becomes unavailable — due to a software bug, interface change, infrastructure failure, or any operational disruption — no liquidations can be processed. Underwater subaccounts accumulate bad debt that cannot be cleared. The protocol's insurance fund (`insurance` in `ClearinghouseLiq`) and socialization mechanism (`perpEngine.socializeSubaccount`) are the only backstops, but these are reactive and bounded. In volatile market conditions where many positions simultaneously breach maintenance margin, the inability to liquidate in a timely manner can cause losses to exceed the insurance fund, resulting in socialized losses across all counterparties.

**Corrupted state delta**: `insurance` balance in `ClearinghouseLiq` is not replenished via `liquidationFees`; `vQuoteBalance` of underwater perp positions is not settled; spot/perp balances of insolvent subaccounts remain open.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Low. Sequencer liveness failures are operationally rare, and market conditions simultaneously requiring urgent liquidations are also infrequent. The combination is low probability, matching the external report's assessment. However, the absence of any on-chain fallback means the window of exposure is unbounded — it lasts for the entire duration of any sequencer outage.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Add `LiquidateSubaccount` to the slow-mode processing path** in `processSlowModeTransactionImpl` so that any user can queue and execute a liquidation after the `SLOW_MODE_TX_DELAY` (3 days) if the sequencer has not acted.
2. **Document the manual sequencer-bypass procedure** for the team, analogous to the external report's runbook recommendation.
3. **Add sequencer health monitoring** with alerting on liquidation queue depth and time-since-last-liquidation metrics.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Step 1 — Sequencer goes offline.** No calls to `submitTransactionsChecked` are made.

**Step 2 — A subaccount becomes underwater.** Oracle price moves cause `isUnderMaintenance(liquidatee)` to return `true` in `ClearinghouseLiq.liquidateSubaccountImpl`.

**Step 3 — Any external liquidator attempts slow-mode submission.** They call `submitSlowModeTransaction` with a `LiquidateSubaccount` payload. `submitSlowModeTransactionImpl` falls through to the `else` branch, charges `SLOW_MODE_FEE`, and queues the transaction.

**Step 4 — After `SLOW_MODE_TX_DELAY` (3 days), liquidator calls `executeSlowModeTransaction`.** This calls `processSlowModeTransaction` → `processSlowModeTransactionImpl`. `LiquidateSubaccount` is not handled; the function hits `revert()`. On mainnet (non-hardhat), `_executeSlowModeTransaction` catches the revert silently. The liquidation never executes. The liquidator loses their `SLOW_MODE_FEE`.

**Step 5 — Underwater position remains open indefinitely**, accumulating bad debt until the sequencer recovers.
